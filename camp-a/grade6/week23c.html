⭐⭐ A2 보통
🔤 A2 Medium
Pinocchio's journey from wooden puppet to real boy is really a journey toward true freedom.
At the beginning he thought freedom meant having no rules and doing whatever he felt like doing.
He discovered through great pain that this kind of freedom always destroys itself in the end.
Real freedom came when he began to make choices he could stand behind honestly and without shame.
The choice to rescue Geppetto from the shark was freely made and freely owned by Pinocchio alone.
Nobody told him he had to do it — he chose it himself because he loved his father completely.
That choice, made with full awareness of the cost, was the freest thing Pinocchio ever did in his life.
Real freedom is not doing whatever we want — it means accepting full responsibility for choices.
⭐⭐⭐ B1 도전
🔤 B1 Challenge
Pinocchio's long journey from wooden puppet to real living boy is really, at its deepest level, a journey toward a true and mature understanding of what freedom actually means.
At the very beginning of his life, he sincerely thought that freedom meant having no rules at all and simply doing whatever he happened to feel like doing at any given moment.
He discovered, slowly and through a great deal of unnecessary pain, that this particular kind of freedom always destroys itself in the end and leaves the person who chose it worse off than before.
Real freedom — the kind of freedom worth having — only came when he began to make choices he could genuinely stand behind honestly and without shame afterward.
The choice to dive into the cold sea and rescue Geppetto from the shark's belly was freely made and freely owned by Pinocchio and Pinocchio alone.
Nobody told him that he had to do it, nobody shamed him into it, nobody stood over him watching — he chose it himself because he loved his father completely and could not imagine leaving him.
That single choice, made with full awareness of its cost and its danger, was without question the freest thing Pinocchio ever did in the course of his entire long life.
It was freedom as responsibility — the exact opposite of what Pleasure Island had pretended freedom was.
Real freedom is not doing whatever we want — it means accepting full responsibility for choices.
