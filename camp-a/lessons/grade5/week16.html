Fogg reached London.
But Fix arrested him at the dock.
The real robber was caught in America.
Fix let Fogg go quickly.
Fogg had lost the wager -- or had he?
Fogg reached Liverpool with a day to spare. But Fix was waiting at the dock with a warrant. He arrested Fogg for bank robbery. For three hours, Fogg sat in a cell while the hours ticked away.
Then Fix burst in: the real bank robber had been caught in America three days earlier. Fogg was innocent. Fix apologised, but the damage was done. The Reform Club closed at midnight -- Fogg was five minutes too late.
He had lost. He sat quietly with Aouda and told her the truth. "There is not a moment to lose," he said gently, "so I will say it now: will you marry me?"
Aouda said yes. And in calculating when to propose, Fogg realised he had made an error in his date calculation -- he had gained a day travelling east. He had not lost after all.
Liverpool. The last port. Fix was on the dock with a warrant, his face a mixture of triumph and discomfort.
Fogg was arrested. He sat in the cell with the precision of a man who knows that panic is useless and calculation is everything. He watched the hours disappear.
Then the door opened. Fix entered, grey-faced. The real robber had been caught in San Francisco. Fogg was not the man. Fix had pursued an innocent person around the world.
"There is not a moment to lose," Fogg said -- and meant it in an entirely different sense from every other time he had used the phrase. He was not talking about travel.
He proposed to Aouda. She accepted. And then Passepartout, doing rapid arithmetic, suddenly seized his master's arm.
"Sir. What day do you think it is?"
It was Saturday, not Sunday. Travelling eastward, Fogg had gained a day. The wager was intact. The Reform Club was still open. They ran.
리버풀. 마지막 항구. 픽스가 영장을 들고 부두에 있었어요. 얼굴에는 승리와 불편함이 뒤섞여 있었어요.
포그는 체포됐어요. 공황이 쓸모없고 계산이 전부라는 것을 아는 사람의 정확성으로 감방에 앉았어요. 시간이 사라지는 것을 지켜봤어요.
그때 문이 열렸어요. 픽스가 창백한 얼굴로 들어왔어요. 진짜 강도는 샌프란시스코에서 잡혔어요. 포그는 그 사람이 아니었어요. 픽스는 무고한 사람을 세계 일주로 쫓아다녔어요.
"1초도 낭비할 수 없어요," 포그가 말했어요 -- 이 문구를 사용한 다른 모든 때와는 완전히 다른 의미로. 여행에 관한 이야기가 아니었어요.
아우다에게 청혼했어요. 그녀가 수락했어요. 그리고 파스파르투가 빠른 계산을 하다가 갑자기 주인의 팔을 잡았어요.
"선생님. 오늘이 무슨 요일인 것 같으세요?"
일요일이 아니라 토요일이었어요. 동쪽으로 여행하면서 포그는 하루를 얻었어요. 내기는 유효했어요. 개혁 클럽은 아직 열려 있었어요. 그들은 달렸어요.

Q2. Fix has followed Fogg around the world and was wrong the entire time. How should we feel about Fix at the end?
Q3. Fogg wins because of a mathematical fact about circumnavigation. Does this feel like a satisfying ending?
Q1. The twist -- the gained day -- was set up by the science of circumnavigation. How does Verne earn this ending rather than simply inventing it?
Q2. Fogg wins the wager but loses the days. He gains a wife and a friend. What does the book ultimately say the journey was for?
Q3. Around the World in Eighty Days was published in 1872. What does it say about the world that a journey once requiring eighty days can now be done in less than twenty hours?
