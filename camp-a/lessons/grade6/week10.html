이야기 읽기
READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
The children made their first wish.
They wanted to be beautiful.
The wish came true.
But nobody knew who they were.
It was a big problem.
The next morning, the children made their first wish. They asked the Psammead to make them beautiful -- the most beautiful children anyone had ever seen.
The wish came true immediately. They looked wonderful. But when they got home, their cook did not recognize them and refused to let them in. Their mother did not know them either.
The wish lasted until sunset. When it wore off, everyone recognized them again. The children had learned their first lesson: a wish can cause more problems than it solves.
The children wasted no time. Their first wish was to be beautiful beyond all imagining -- and the Psammead, grumbling as always, granted it.
They were stunning. Strangers stopped to stare. But when they reached home, the cook took one look at them and slammed the door. Their own house would not let them in. Their mother, returning from an errand, walked straight past them.
They spent the afternoon outside, cold and hungry, waiting for sunset.
When the wish wore off at dusk, they tumbled in through the door, relieved. But the Psammead had made its point: beauty without context is useless, and wishes always have a price.
아이들은 시간을 낭비하지 않았어요. 첫 번째 소원은 상상을 초월할 만큼 아름다워지는 것이었어요 -- 모래요정은 언제나처럼 투덜거리며 들어줬어요.
그들은 놀라웠어요. 낯선 사람들이 멈춰서 바라봤어요. 하지만 집에 도착했을 때, 요리사는 그들을 한 번 보고 문을 쾅 닫았어요. 자신들의 집이 들여보내주지 않았어요. 심부름에서 돌아온 엄마도 그들 곁을 그냥 지나갔어요.
오후 내내 밖에서 춥고 배고프게 해질 녘을 기다렸어요.
황혼이 되어 소원이 사라지자, 문을 통해 안도하며 들어왔어요. 하지만 모래요정은 자신의 요점을 분명히 했어요: 맥락 없는 아름다움은 쓸모없고, 소원에는 항상 대가가 있어요.
