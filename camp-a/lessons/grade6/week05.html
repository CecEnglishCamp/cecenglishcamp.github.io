READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
Sara's father put his money in diamond mines.
He hoped to get very rich.
But the mines were not good.
Sara missed her father very much.
Sara's father, Captain Crewe, put almost all his money into diamond mines in India. He hoped the mines would make him very rich.
At first, he wrote Sara happy letters. But slowly, his letters became shorter and more worried.
The mines were not going well. He was spending more money than he was making.
Miss Minchin did not know about this. Sara did not fully understand either.
She just waited for her father's letters every week and missed him very much.
Sara's comfortable life at school depended on her father's money — and her father's money depended on one big decision.
Captain Crewe had put almost all his money into diamond mines in India, together with an old friend. At first it seemed like a very good plan.
But the mines were not producing enough, and costs kept going up. Captain Crewe's letters to Sara became shorter. Something in them felt different — less happy, more worried.
Miss Minchin noticed nothing. Sara felt that something was wrong, but she did not know how serious it was. She just kept waiting for his next letter.
사라의 편안한 학교 생활은 아버지의 돈에 달려 있었어요 — 그리고 아버지의 돈은 한 가지 큰 결정에 달려 있었어요.
크루 선장은 오래된 친구와 함께 거의 모든 돈을 인도의 다이아몬드 광산에 투자했어요. 처음에는 매우 좋은 계획처럼 보였어요.
하지만 광산은 충분히 생산하지 못했고, 비용은 계속 올라갔어요. 사라에게 보내는 크루 선장의 편지는 점점 짧아졌어요. 편지에는 뭔가 달라진 것이 느껴졌어요 — 덜 행복하고, 더 걱정스러워졌어요.
미스 민친은 아무것도 알아채지 못했어요. 사라는 뭔가 잘못됐다는 느낌이 들었지만, 얼마나 심각한지는 몰랐어요. 그냥 아버지의 다음 편지를 기다렸어요.

Q1. The story says "something in them felt different — less happy, more worried." Sara noticed this through letters alone. What does this tell us about her?
Q2. Captain Crewe invested "almost all his money" in one venture. In what way is this connected to his character as we have seen it?
Q3. At this point, Sara senses trouble but does not know how bad it will become. How might this dramatic irony — where the reader suspects more than Sara does — affect how we read the story?
