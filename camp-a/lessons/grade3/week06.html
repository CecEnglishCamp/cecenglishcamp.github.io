Peter ran all the way home. He was very tired and felt sick.
His mother was waiting at the door. Peter had lost his jacket and shoes.
His mother looked at him quietly. She could see that Peter was not well.
She put him to bed and made him a warm cup of chamomile tea.
His three sisters ate bread and berries for supper. Peter lay in bed and thought about his long, difficult day.
Peter ran home without stopping. When he arrived, his legs were shaking and his face was pale.
Mrs. Rabbit saw him come through the door — no jacket, no shoes, and not well at all. She asked no questions. She could see what had happened.
She put Peter to bed and brought him a cup of warm chamomile tea. Peter drank it without a word.
In the kitchen, his three sisters were happily eating berries and bread and laughing together. Peter could hear them. He understood exactly why their evening was so different from his.
Peter ran home without stopping. His legs were shaking. Mother saw him — no jacket, no shoes.
She put him to bed and brought him warm chamomile tea. Peter drank it without a word.
His sisters were laughing in the kitchen. They had stayed in the meadow all day and done nothing wrong.
