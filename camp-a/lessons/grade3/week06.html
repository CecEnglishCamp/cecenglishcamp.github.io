이야기 읽기
READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
Peter ran home fast.
He was very tired.
He was not well.

Mother saw Peter.
He had no jacket and no shoes.
Peter went straight to bed.
Mother gave him some tea.
His sisters had bread and berries.
Peter ran all the way home without stopping. When he arrived, he was exhausted and felt very sick. His mother was waiting at the door.

Peter had lost his jacket and his shoes in Mr. McGregor's garden. His mother did not say much. She could see that Peter was not well.
Peter was not well. He went straight to bed.
Mrs. Rabbit made him a cup of chamomile tea. It was warm and soothing.
His three sisters had a much better day. They ate bread and blackberries for supper.
Peter did not stop running until he reached home. Even then, his legs barely held him. He collapsed through the door pale, shivering, and missing both his jacket and shoes.

Mrs. Rabbit asked no questions. She had eyes; she could see. She put Peter to bed with a cup of chamomile tea, which he drank without a word.
Peter was not well. He went straight to bed, and lay staring at the ceiling.
In the kitchen, Flopsy, Mopsy, and Cotton-tail were eating blackberries and warm bread. They had done nothing wrong, and were rewarded accordingly. Peter was aware of the contrast — and that, perhaps, was punishment enough.
피터는 집에 도착할 때까지 멈추지 않고 달렸어요. 다리가 겨우 버텼어요. 그는 창백하고 떨리는 몸으로 문을 통해 쓰러졌어요 — 재킷과 신발은 맥그리거 씨의 정원에 남겨두고요.

토끼 엄마는 아무것도 묻지 않았어요. 눈이 있으니 볼 수 있었죠. 그녀는 피터를 침대에 눕히고 캐모마일 차 한 잔을 만들어줬어요. 부엌에서 세 자매는 블랙베리와 따뜻한 빵을 먹고 있었어요. 그들은 아무 잘못도 하지 않았고 그에 따른 보상을 받았어요. 피터는 그 차이를 알고 있었어요 — 어쩌면 그것으로 충분한 벌이었을지도요.
