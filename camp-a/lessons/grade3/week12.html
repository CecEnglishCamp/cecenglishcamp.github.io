이야기 읽기
READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
Jack and his mother were very poor.
Mother said: sell the cow.
Jack went to the market.
He met an old man.
He traded the cow for magic beans.
Jack and his mother were very poor. They had nothing left to sell except their old cow, Bessie.
"Take Bessie to the market and sell her," said Mother sadly.
On the road, Jack met a strange old man. The man offered five magic beans for the cow. Jack thought this was a wonderful deal and traded Bessie for the beans.
When he got home and showed his mother, she was furious. "Magic beans!" she cried. She threw them out of the window into the garden. They went to bed hungry that night.
Poverty had stripped Jack's home down to almost nothing. Their last asset was the old cow, Bessie, and Mother had made the reluctant decision to sell her.
Jack set off for market. On the road he met a peculiar old man who seemed to know the cow's name and offered five beans in exchange -- magic beans, he insisted, worth more than money.
Jack accepted. He was certain it was a brilliant trade.
His mother did not share his certainty. She threw the beans out of the window in fury and sent Jack to bed without supper. The beans landed in the dark soil of the garden. Something extraordinary was about to begin.
가난이 잭의 집을 거의 아무것도 없는 상태로 만들었어요. 마지막 자산은 늙은 소 베시였고, 엄마는 팔아야 한다는 힘든 결정을 내렸어요.
잭은 시장으로 향했어요. 길에서 소의 이름을 알고 있는 듯한 이상한 노인을 만났어요. 노인은 콩 다섯 개를 소와 바꾸자고 했어요 -- 마법의 콩이라고 주장하며, 돈보다 훨씬 가치가 있다고 했어요.
잭은 수락했어요. 훌륭한 거래라고 확신했어요.
엄마는 그의 확신을 나누지 않았어요. 화가 나서 콩을 창밖으로 던져버리고 잭을 저녁도 안 먹이고 잠자리에 들게 했어요. 콩은 정원의 어두운 흙에 떨어졌어요. 놀라운 일이 시작되려 하고 있었어요.

이해 문제
COMPREHENSION · 수준을 골라 도전해봐요
Q1. Why did Jack go to the market?
Q2. What did Jack get in exchange for the cow?
Q3. How did Mother react to the beans?
Q1. The old man seemed to know the cow's name. Why is this detail important?
Q2. Jack was certain it was a brilliant trade. Mother was furious. Who was right?
Q3. Mother throws away the beans without knowing what they are. What does this tell us about her character?
Q1. Jack makes a decision that looks foolish but turns out to be inspired. What does this say about the nature of fairy tale heroes?
Q2. The beans land in the soil of the garden. Why does the story end the chapter here, just before something happens?
Q3. Poverty is the starting condition of many fairy tales. Why?
