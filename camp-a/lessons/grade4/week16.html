READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
Dorothy woke up in Kansas.
Aunt Em was beside her bed.
She told everyone about Oz.
Nobody believed her.
But Dorothy knew it was real.
Dorothy opened her eyes. She was in her own bed in Kansas. Aunt Em was leaning over her, worried and relieved at the same time.
"I've been to a wonderful place called Oz," Dorothy said. "There were scarecrows and tin men and lions and witches and a wizard behind a curtain!"
Aunt Em smiled kindly but clearly did not believe a word of it. Dorothy looked around the grey farmhouse. After all the colour of Oz, it seemed very plain.
But then she looked at the faces of people she loved, and she understood something she had not understood before.
"There's no place like home," Dorothy said again -- and this time she truly meant it.
The farmhouse. The smell of it. Aunt Em's hand on her forehead, cool and familiar.
Dorothy tried to explain Oz -- the yellow road, the green city, the companions, the Wizard's curtain, Glinda's kindness. She could hear how it sounded. Aunt Em made soothing noises and suggested rest.
Dorothy looked around the room she had known all her life. After the extraordinary colour and scale of Oz, it was undeniably grey and small. She had wanted nothing more than to return to it. And now she was here, and it was exactly as grey and small as she remembered.
And yet.
The faces around her were faces she knew. The sounds were sounds she recognised. The smell of the farm -- hay and earth and something baking -- was the smell of safety.
"There's no place like home," Dorothy said quietly. She understood it now in a way she never could have before she left.
농가. 그 냄새. 이모 엠의 손이 이마에 닿았어요, 시원하고 익숙하게.
도로시는 오즈를 설명하려 했어요 -- 노란 길, 초록 도시, 동반자들, 마법사의 커튼, 글린다의 친절. 어떻게 들릴지 느껴졌어요. 이모 엠은 달래는 소리를 내며 쉬라고 했어요.
도로시는 평생 알아온 방을 둘러봤어요. 오즈의 놀라운 색깔과 규모 뒤에, 틀림없이 회색이고 작았어요. 돌아오고 싶은 것이 없었어요. 이제 여기 있었고, 기억했던 것만큼 정확히 회색이고 작았어요.
하지만.
주변의 얼굴들은 아는 얼굴이었어요. 소리들은 알아들을 수 있는 소리였어요. 농장의 냄새 -- 건초와 흙과 뭔가 구워지는 것 -- 는 안전의 냄새였어요.
"집만 한 곳은 없어요," 도로시가 조용히 말했어요. 떠나기 전에는 절대 알 수 없었던 방식으로 이제 이해했어요.
