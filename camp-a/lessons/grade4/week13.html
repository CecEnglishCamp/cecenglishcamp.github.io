READING · 수준을 골라 읽어보세요
The Wicked Witch sent her flying monkeys.
They captured Dorothy and her friends.
Dorothy was taken to the witch's castle.
The witch wanted her ruby slippers.
Dorothy threw water on the witch.
The Wicked Witch of the West was furious that Dorothy had killed her sister. She sent her winged monkeys to capture Dorothy and her friends. The Tin Man and the Lion were taken prisoner. Dorothy was brought to the witch's dark castle.
The witch wanted Dorothy's magical ruby slippers. She threatened and frightened her. But Dorothy would not give them up.
When the witch set the Scarecrow on fire, Dorothy grabbed a bucket of water to put it out. She threw it -- and some of the water hit the witch.
"I'm melting! I'm melting!" the witch screamed. She disappeared into a puddle on the floor.
"You have no power here!" Dorothy said quietly.
The Wicked Witch of the West had one objective: the ruby slippers on Dorothy's feet. They had belonged to her sister, and she wanted them back with a cold, absolute fury.
She sent her winged monkeys -- creatures that obeyed without question -- and they delivered Dorothy to the castle with the efficiency of a military operation. The Lion and the Tin Man were caged. The Scarecrow was torn apart and scattered across a field.
In the castle, the witch threatened, pleaded, and raged. The slippers would not come off Dorothy's feet. They never would, not while Dorothy was alive.
Then the witch set the Scarecrow's arm alight. Dorothy grabbed a bucket of water. In her panic, she threw it wide -- and it hit the witch full in the face.
"You have no power here," Dorothy said, almost to herself. It was simply what she believed.
서쪽의 나쁜 마녀에게는 한 가지 목표가 있었어요: 도로시의 발에 있는 루비 구두. 동생의 것이었고, 차갑고 절대적인 분노로 되찾고 싶었어요.
마녀는 날개 달린 원숭이들을 보냈어요 -- 군사 작전의 효율성으로 도로시를 성에 데려다줬어요. 사자와 양철 나무꾼은 우리에 갇혔어요. 허수아비는 찢겨서 들판에 흩어졌어요.
성에서 마녀는 협박하고, 애원하고, 격노했어요. 구두는 도로시의 발에서 벗겨지지 않았어요. 도로시가 살아있는 한 절대 벗겨지지 않을 거예요.
그때 마녀가 허수아비의 팔에 불을 붙였어요. 도로시가 물 양동이를 잡았어요. 당황해서 빗나갔는데 -- 마녀의 얼굴에 정통으로 맞았어요.
"당신은 여기서 아무 힘도 없어요," 도로시가 거의 혼자 말하듯 했어요. 단순히 그녀가 믿는 것이었어요.
