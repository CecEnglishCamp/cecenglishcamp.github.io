G22: Present Perfect Simple

A2 Grammar Lesson – 현재완료 시제

기본 개념 (Concept)

현재완료 시제(Present Perfect Simple)는 과거의 행동이나 경험이 현재와 연결될 때 사용합니다. 단순 과거와 달리, 시간보다 결과나 경험에 초점을 둡니다.

have/has + 과거분사(past participle)

예: I have eaten lunch. (나는 점심을 먹었어 → 지금 배가 안 고픔)

주요 용법은 다음과 같습니다:

  • 경험: I have been to Paris. (파리에 가본 경험이 있어요)
  • 결과: She has broken her leg. (다리를 다쳐서 지금도 아파요)
  • 변화: We have grown a lot. (우리가 많이 자랐어요)
  • 완료: They have finished the project. (프로젝트를 완료했어요)

문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

긍정문 (Affirmative)

  • I have visited London. (나는 런던을 방문한 적이 있어요)
  • She has read that book. (그녀는 그 책을 읽었어요)
  • We have cleaned the room. (우리는 방을 청소했어요)
  • He has learned English. (그는 영어를 배웠어요)
  • They have arrived. (그들이 도착했어요)
  • You have improved a lot. (당신이 많이 발전했어요)
  • My dog has eaten already. (우리 강아지는 이미 밥을 먹었어요)

부정문 (Negative)

  • I haven't visited London. (나는 런던을 방문한 적이 없어요)
  • She hasn't read that book. (그녀는 그 책을 읽지 않았어요)
  • We haven't cleaned the room. (우리는 방을 청소하지 않았어요)
  • He hasn't learned English. (그는 영어를 배우지 않았어요)
  • They haven't arrived. (그들이 아직 도착하지 않았어요)
  • You haven't improved yet. (당신이 아직 발전하지 않았어요)
  • My dog hasn't eaten today. (우리 강아지가 오늘 밥을 안 먹었어요)

의문문 (Interrogative)

  • Have you visited London? (런던을 방문한 적이 있나요?)
  • Has she read that book? (그녀가 그 책을 읽었나요?)
  • Have we cleaned the room? (우리가 방을 청소했나요?)
  • Has he learned English? (그가 영어를 배웠나요?)
  • Have they arrived? (그들이 도착했나요?)
  • Have you improved? (당신이 발전했나요?)
  • Has your dog eaten? (당신 강아지가 밥을 먹었나요?)

긍정문 예시 (Positive Examples)

경험 (Experience)

  • I have traveled to Japan. (나는 일본에 여행간 적이 있어요)
  • She has tried sushi. (그녀는 스시를 먹어본 적이 있어요)
  • We have met before. (우리는 전에 만난 적이 있어요)
  • He has seen that movie. (그는 그 영화를 본 적이 있어요)
  • They have visited the museum. (그들은 박물관을 방문한 적이 있어요)

결과 (Result)

  • He has lost his keys. (그가 열쇠를 잃어버렸어요 - 지금도 없음)
  • They have broken the window. (그들이 창문을 깼어요 - 지금도 깨져있음)
  • I have hurt my hand. (손을 다쳤어요 - 지금도 아픔)
  • She has cut her hair. (머리를 잘랐어요 - 지금 짧음)

변화 (Change)

  • You have grown taller. (키가 더 커졌네요)
  • My English has improved. (제 영어가 늘었어요)
  • The weather has changed. (날씨가 변했어요)
  • She has become confident. (그녀가 자신감이 생겼어요)

완료 (Completion)

  • We have finished our homework. (우리는 숙제를 끝냈어요)
  • I have cleaned the kitchen. (부엌을 청소했어요)
  • He has written the report. (보고서를 작성했어요)
  • They have completed the project. (프로젝트를 완료했어요)

부정문 예시 (Negative Examples)

경험 없음 (No Experience)

  • I haven't been to Canada. (캐나다에 가본 적이 없어요)
  • She hasn't tried kimchi. (그녀는 김치를 먹어본 적이 없어요)
  • We haven't met his family. (우리는 그의 가족을 만난 적이 없어요)
  • He hasn't seen snow. (그는 눈을 본 적이 없어요)

완료되지 않음 (Not Completed)

  • We haven't finished yet. (우리는 아직 끝내지 못했어요)
  • He hasn't arrived. (그가 아직 도착하지 않았어요)
  • I haven't done my homework. (숙제를 하지 않았어요)
  • They haven't called back. (그들이 아직 전화를 하지 않았어요)

변화 없음 (No Change)

  • You haven't changed much. (별로 변하지 않았네요)
  • My skills haven't improved. (제 실력이 늘지 않았어요)
  • The situation hasn't gotten better. (상황이 나아지지 않았어요)

결과 없음 (No Result)

  • They haven't broken anything. (그들은 아무것도 깨뜨리지 않았어요)
  • I haven't lost my wallet. (지갑을 잃어버리지 않았어요)
  • She hasn't hurt herself. (다치지 않았어요)

의문문 예시 (Question Examples)

  • A: Have you ever been to New York? (뉴욕에 가본 적 있어요?)
    B: Yes, I have. I went last year. (네, 있어요. 작년에 갔어요)
  • A: Has she finished her homework? (그녀가 숙제를 끝냈나요?)
    B: No, she hasn't. She's still working on it. (아니요. 아직 하고 있어요)
  • A: Have they arrived yet? (그들이 벌써 도착했나요?)
    B: Yes, they have. They're in the lobby. (네, 로비에 있어요)
  • A: Have you eaten lunch? (점심 드셨어요?)
    B: Not yet. I'm about to. (아직요. 지금 먹으려고요)
  • A: Has he called you? (그가 전화했나요?)
    B: No, he hasn't. I'm waiting. (아니요. 기다리고 있어요)
  • A: Have we met before? (전에 만난 적이 있나요?)
    B: I think we have, at the conference. (컨퍼런스에서 만난 것 같아요)
  • A: Have you tried this cake? (이 케이크 드셔봤어요?)
    B: Yes, it's delicious! (네, 정말 맛있어요!)
  • A: Has your brother traveled abroad? (형/동생이 해외여행 가본 적 있나요?)
    B: Yes, he has been to Germany and Japan. (네, 독일과 일본에 가봤어요)
  • A: Have you heard the news? (그 소식 들었어요?)
    B: No, what happened? (아니요, 무슨 일이에요?)
  • A: Have they cleaned the classroom? (교실을 청소했나요?)
    B: Yes, it's spotless now. (네, 지금 완전 깨끗해요)
  • A: Has she ever ridden a horse? (말을 타본 적이 있나요?)
    B: No, she hasn't. She's afraid of animals. (없어요. 동물을 무서워해요)
  • A: Have you finished the report? (보고서 끝냈어요?)
    B: Almost. I just need to add the summary. (거의요. 요약만 추가하면 돼요)

상황별 대화 흐름 (Situational Dialogues)

학교 상황 (School Situation)
A: Have you submitted your assignment?
B: Yes, I have. I sent it this morning.
A: Has the teacher checked it?
B: Not yet. She said she'll review it today.
A: Have you ever missed a deadline?
B: No, I haven't. I always finish early.

여행 상황 (Travel Situation)
A: Have you ever traveled alone?
B: Yes, I have. I went to Thailand last summer.
A: Have you visited any temples there?
B: I have. The architecture was amazing.
A: Has your family traveled with you before?
B: No, they haven't. They prefer local trips.

직장 상황 (Work Situation)
A: Have you completed the presentation?
B: Yes, I have. It's ready to share.
A: Has the manager seen it?
B: Not yet. I'll send it after lunch.
A: Have you ever presented to clients?
B: Yes, I have. It's challenging but rewarding.

온라인 상황 (Online Situation)
A: Have you uploaded the video?
B: Yes, I have. It's live now.
A: Have you checked the comments?
B: Not yet. I'll do that next.
A: Has anyone shared it?
B: Yes, three people already have.

가족 상황 (Family Situation)
A: Have you cleaned your room?
B: Yes, I have. It's finally tidy.
A: Has Mom seen it?
B: Not yet. I want to surprise her.
A: Have you done the dishes too?
B: No, I haven't. That's next.

병원 상황 (Hospital Situation)
A: Have you taken your medicine?
B: Yes, I have. I feel better now.
A: Has the doctor called back?
B: Not yet. I'm still waiting.
A: Have you ever had this pain before?
B: No, I haven't. It's new.

흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

  • ❌ I have went to school. → ✅ I have gone to school.
  • ❌ She has eat lunch. → ✅ She has eaten lunch.
  • ❌ Have you saw it? → ✅ Have you seen it?
  • ❌ He haven't finished. → ✅ He hasn't finished.
  • ❌ We has cleaned. → ✅ We have cleaned.
  • ❌ They have did it. → ✅ They have done it.
  • ❌ I have been yesterday. → ✅ I went yesterday. (현재완료는 특정 과거 시간과 함께 쓰지 않음)
  • ❌ She have wrote a letter. → ✅ She has written a letter.
  • ❌ Have he come? → ✅ Has he come?
  • ❌ I have buy a car. → ✅ I have bought a car.

레벨업 팁 (Level-Up Tips)

  • 현재완료는 경험, 결과, 변화, 완료를 표현할 때 사용됩니다.
  • yet, already, ever, never, just 같은 시간 표현과 자주 함께 사용됩니다.
  • 특정 과거 시간 표현 (yesterday, last week 등)과는 함께 쓰지 않습니다.
  • 의문문에서는 Have/Has가 문장 맨 앞에 옵니다.
  • 과거분사 형태를 정확히 외우는 것이 중요합니다.
  • 현재완료는 현재와 연결된 과거를 강조합니다.
  • for와 since를 구분해서 사용하세요: for + 기간, since + 시점

비교 표현 (Compare with Other Tenses)

  • 현재완료 vs 과거시제:
    I have seen that movie. (그 영화를 본 경험이 있어요)
    I saw that movie last night. (어젯밤에 그 영화를 봤어요 - 특정 시간)
  • 현재완료 vs 현재진행:
    I have cleaned the room. (방을 청소했어요 - 완료)
    I am cleaning the room. (방을 청소하고 있어요 - 진행 중)
  • 현재완료 vs 현재시제:
    I have lived here for 5 years. (5년 동안 여기 살고 있어요)
    I live here. (여기 살아요 - 현재 상태)
  • 현재완료 vs 과거완료:
    I have finished my homework. (숙제를 끝냈어요 - 지금 완료됨)
    I had finished my homework before dinner. (저녁 전에 숙제를 끝냈었어요)
  • 현재완료 vs 미래:
    I have done it. (이미 완료했어요)
    I will do it. (할 예정이에요)

발음 가이드 (Pronunciation Guide)

Have/Has + 과거분사는 빠르게 말할 때 축약되거나 연결음으로 들릴 수 있습니다.

축약형 (Contractions)

  • I've seen it. → [aɪv ˈsiːn ɪt]
  • She's gone. → [ʃiːz ɡɔːn]
  • We've finished. → [wiːv ˈfɪnɪʃt]
  • He's eaten. → [hiːz ˈiːtn]
  • They've arrived. → [ðeɪv əˈraɪvd]

관련 어휘 50개 (Common Past Participles)

일상 동작 (Daily Actions)

gone, come, done, made, had, seen, heard, eaten, drunk, slept

학업/지식 (Study/Knowledge)

learned, studied, understood, written, read, explained, taught, memorized, solved, answered

기술/작업 (Skills/Work)

built, fixed, opened, closed, cleaned, painted, typed, coded, uploaded, downloaded

감정/경험 (Emotions/Experience)

felt, loved, hated, enjoyed, missed, cried, smiled, laughed, worried, relaxed

이동/여행 (Movement/Travel)

traveled, flown, driven, walked, run, climbed, stayed, visited, returned, left

연습문제 (Practice Exercises)

객관식 (Multiple Choice)

  1. Which is correct?
    A. I have went. B. I have gone.
  2. Choose the correct question:
    A. Has she eat? B. Has she eaten?
  3. Fill in: They ___ arrived.
    A. have B. has
  4. Which is present perfect?
    A. I went yesterday. B. I have been there.
  5. Complete: She ___ never ___ sushi.
    A. has/tried B. have/try

주관식 (Short Answer)

  1. Write 3 things you have done today.
  2. Write 3 things you haven't done this week.
  3. Make 2 questions using "Have you ever...?"
  4. Translate: 나는 그 영화를 본 적 있어요.
