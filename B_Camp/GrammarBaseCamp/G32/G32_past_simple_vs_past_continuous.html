G32: Past Simple vs Past Continuous

A2 Grammar Lesson – 과거 시제 비교

기본 개념 (Concept)

Past Simple은 과거의 완료된 행동이나 사건을 표현합니다. Past Continuous는 과거의 특정 시점에 진행 중이던 행동을 나타냅니다.

PAST SIMPLE

완료된 행동

시작과 끝이 명확한 과거 사건

주어 + 과거형 동사

PAST CONTINUOUS

진행 중이던 행동

과거 특정 시점의 진행 상황

주어 + was/were + -ing

시간선에서 이해하기:

Past Continuous: ────●────●───── (진행 중)

Past Simple: ────────▲───────── (완료된 순간)

예시 비교:

I watched a movie last night. (Past Simple – 완료된 행동)
I was watching a movie when you called. (Past Continuous – 진행 중이던 행동)
She cooked dinner yesterday. (요리를 완료함)
She was cooking when I arrived. (도착했을 때 요리 중이었음)

문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

Past Simple 구조

  • 긍정문: 주어 + 과거형 동사 (규칙/불규칙)
  • 부정문: 주어 + didn't + 동사원형
  • 의문문: Did + 주어 + 동사원형?
  • 예시: I walked / I didn't walk / Did I walk?
  • 시간 표현: yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago, when

Past Continuous 구조

  • 긍정문: 주어 + was/were + 동사-ing
  • 부정문: 주어 + wasn't/weren't + 동사-ing
  • 의문문: Was/Were + 주어 + 동사-ing?
  • 예시: I was walking / I wasn't walking / Was I walking?
  • 시간 표현: at 5pm, while, when, all evening, during

시제 결합 패턴

  • Past Continuous + Past Simple: I was cooking when he arrived.
  • While + Past Continuous: While I was reading, the lights went out.
  • When + Past Simple: When I entered, they were talking.
  • 두 개의 Past Continuous: I was studying while she was cooking.

사용 상황

  • Past Simple: 완료된 사건, 순서가 있는 사건들, 습관
  • Past Continuous: 배경 설명, 중단된 행동, 동시 진행 행동

긍정문 예시 (Positive Examples)

Past Simple - 완료된 행동들

  • I visited my grandma yesterday. (어제 할머니를 방문했어요)
  • She cooked dinner last night. (어젯밤 저녁을 요리했어요)
  • We watched a movie on Friday. (금요일에 영화를 봤어요)
  • They played soccer after school. (방과 후 축구를 했어요)
  • He finished his homework early. (숙제를 일찍 끝냈어요)
  • I met my friend at the café. (카페에서 친구를 만났어요)
  • She traveled to Japan in 2022. (2022년에 일본에 갔어요)
  • We cleaned the house last weekend. (지난 주말에 집을 청소했어요)
  • They studied for the test. (시험 공부를 했어요)
  • He called me in the morning. (아침에 전화했어요)

Past Continuous - 진행 중이던 행동들

  • I was reading a book at 8pm. (8시에 책을 읽고 있었어요)
  • She was cooking when I arrived. (내가 도착했을 때 요리하고 있었어요)
  • We were walking in the park. (공원에서 산책하고 있었어요)
  • They were studying all evening. (저녁 내내 공부하고 있었어요)
  • He was driving to work. (직장으로 운전해 가고 있었어요)
  • I was listening to music. (음악을 듣고 있었어요)
  • She was talking on the phone. (전화 통화를 하고 있었어요)
  • We were watching TV when the power went out. (정전됐을 때 TV를 보고 있었어요)
  • They were playing video games. (비디오 게임을 하고 있었어요)
  • He was writing an email. (이메일을 쓰고 있었어요)

시제 혼합 예시

  • I was sleeping when the alarm rang. (알람이 울렸을 때 자고 있었어요)
  • She was working while he was cooking. (그가 요리하는 동안 일하고 있었어요)
  • We were talking when the teacher entered. (선생님이 들어왔을 때 대화하고 있었어요)
  • He called while I was taking a shower. (내가 샤워하는 동안 전화했어요)
  • They were eating when it started to rain. (비가 시작됐을 때 식사하고 있었어요)

의문문 예시 (Question Examples)

Past Simple 의문문

  • A: Did you go to the party? (파티에 갔어요?)
    B: Yes, I went with Sarah. (네, 사라와 함께 갔어요)
  • A: Did she finish the report? (보고서를 끝냈나요?)
    B: No, she's still working on it. (아니요, 아직 작업 중이에요)
  • A: Did they watch the movie? (영화를 봤나요?)
    B: Yes, they loved it. (네, 정말 좋아했어요)
  • A: Did he call you yesterday? (어제 전화했나요?)
    B: No, I didn't hear from him. (아니요, 연락이 없었어요)
  • A: Did you study last night? (어젯밤 공부했어요?)
    B: Yes, for three hours. (네, 세 시간 동안요)
  • A: Did she travel last summer? (작년 여름에 여행갔나요?)
    B: Yes, to Europe. (네, 유럽에 갔어요)

Past Continuous 의문문

  • A: Were you sleeping at 10pm? (10시에 자고 있었어요?)
    B: No, I was watching TV. (아니요, TV를 보고 있었어요)
  • A: Was she cooking when you arrived? (당신이 도착했을 때 요리하고 있었나요?)
    B: Yes, she was making pasta. (네, 파스타를 만들고 있었어요)
  • A: Were they playing soccer? (축구를 하고 있었나요?)
    B: Yes, in the park. (네, 공원에서요)
  • A: Was he working all day? (하루 종일 일하고 있었나요?)
    B: No, he took a break. (아니요, 휴식을 취했어요)
  • A: Were you listening to music? (음악을 듣고 있었어요?)
    B: Yes, with my headphones. (네, 헤드폰으로요)
  • A: Was she talking to her friend? (친구와 이야기하고 있었나요?)
    B: Yes, on the phone. (네, 전화로요)

상황별 대화 흐름 (Situational Dialogues)

학교 상황 (School Situation)
A: Did you study for the test?
B: Yes, I studied last night.
A: What were you doing at 9pm?
B: I was reviewing grammar.
A: Was it difficult?
B: A little, but I understood most of it.

여행 상황 (Travel Situation)
A: Did you visit the museum?
B: Yes, I went there yesterday.
A: Were you walking when it started to rain?
B: Yes, I was walking near the river.
A: What did you do then?
B: I found a café and waited.

직장 상황 (Work Situation)
A: Did you finish the report?
B: Yes, I sent it this morning.
A: What were you doing at 10am?
B: I was meeting with the team.
A: Were you discussing the project?
B: Yes, we made important decisions.

가정 상황 (Home Situation)
A: Did Mom cook dinner?
B: Yes, she made pasta.
A: What were you doing while she cooked?
B: I was setting the table.
A: Were you helping her?
B: Yes, I prepared the salad too.

친구와의 대화 (With Friends)
A: What did you do last weekend?
B: I visited my cousin in Seoul.
A: Were you having fun there?
B: Yes, we were exploring the city.
A: Did you take any photos?
B: Lots! I'll show you later.

문장 결합 연습 (Sentence Combining)

두 개의 과거 행동을 자연스럽게 연결하는 연습입니다:

I was walking. + It started to rain.
→ I was walking when it started to rain.
She was cooking. + The phone rang.
→ She was cooking when the phone rang.
  • We were studying. + The lights went out. → We were studying when the lights went out.
  • He was driving. + He saw an accident. → He was driving when he saw an accident.
  • I was sleeping. + You called me. → I was sleeping when you called me.
  • They were playing soccer. + It started to snow. → They were playing soccer when it started to snow.
  • I was reading. + The doorbell rang. → I was reading when the doorbell rang.
  • She was talking. + Her phone died. → She was talking when her phone died.
  • We were walking. + We saw a rainbow. → We were walking when we saw a rainbow.
  • He was working. + His computer crashed. → He was working when his computer crashed.
  • I was watching TV. + The power went out. → I was watching TV when the power went out.
  • They were eating. + The fire alarm went off. → They were eating when the fire alarm went off.

실생활 사용 예시 (Real-Life Usage)

이메일 보고

I finished the report yesterday. I was writing it when the manager called to ask about the deadline.

일기 작성

Yesterday was strange. I was walking home when I saw a deer in the middle of the street. It just stood there for a moment, then ran away.

전화 대화 중 설명

Sorry I didn't answer earlier. I was taking a shower when you called. I heard the phone but couldn't reach it in time.

뉴스 리포트

The accident happened at 3pm. Traffic was moving slowly when a truck hit the bridge. No one was injured, but the road was closed for two hours.

수업 중 설명

We were discussing grammar when the fire alarm rang. Everyone left the building calmly, and we continued the lesson later.

자주 쓰이는 표현 패턴 (Common Patterns)

  • Past Simple + time marker: I visited Paris last summer.
  • Past Continuous + specific time: I was studying at 8pm.
  • When + Past Simple (interruption): I was cooking when he arrived.
  • While + Past Continuous (background): While I was reading, the lights went out.
  • Was/Were + verb-ing: She was talking to her friend.
  • Didn't + base verb: I didn't go to the party.
  • Wasn't/Weren't + verb-ing: I wasn't sleeping at that time.
  • Sequential actions: He woke up, brushed his teeth, and left.
  • Interrupted action: I was walking when it started to rain.
  • Parallel actions: I was cooking while she was cleaning.

흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

  • ❌ I was go to school → ✅ I was going to school
  • ❌ She was cook dinner → ✅ She was cooking dinner
  • ❌ They was playing soccer → ✅ They were playing soccer
  • ❌ I was sleep when you called → ✅ I was sleeping when you called
  • ❌ Did you went to the party? → ✅ Did you go to the party?
  • ❌ He didn't went home → ✅ He didn't go home
  • ❌ Was you working? → ✅ Were you working?
  • ❌ I didn't was sleeping → ✅ I wasn't sleeping
  • ❌ She was called me → ✅ She called me / She was calling me
  • ❌ We was watch TV → ✅ We were watching TV

레벨업 팁 (Level-Up Tips)

  • Past Simple: 완료된 행동, 명확한 시간 표현과 함께 사용
  • Past Continuous: 진행 중이던 행동, 배경 설명에 사용
  • When: 짧은 행동이 긴 행동을 끊을 때 사용 (interruption)
  • While: 두 행동이 동시에 진행될 때 사용 (parallel action)
  • Past Continuous는 be동사 + 동사-ing 형태를 정확히 써야 함
  • 불규칙 동사 과거형은 반드시 암기 필요 (go → went, eat → ate)
  • 이야기할 때 두 시제를 섞어 쓰면 더 자연스러운 흐름을 만들 수 있음
  • 상황의 배경은 Past Continuous, 주요 사건은 Past Simple로 설명
