1. 기본 개념 (Basic Concept)

RCRELATIVE CLAUSES

사람, 사물, 장소, 시간에 대해 추가 정보를 주는 문장
관계대명사(who, which, that, whose, where, when)로 연결됨
두 개의 단순한 문장을 하나의 복잡한 문장으로 결합

Simple: The man called you. He is my uncle.

Combined: The man who called you is my uncle.

Simple: My sister lives in Paris. She is visiting.

Combined: My sister, who lives in Paris, is visiting.

Defining vs Non-Defining Clauses

Defining (제한 관계절)

필수 정보 - 문장의 의미를 결정함

쉼표 없음

that 사용 가능

The man who called you is my uncle.

Non-Defining (비제한 관계절)

추가 정보 - 없어도 문장 의미 유지

쉼표로 구분

that 사용 불가

My sister, who lives in Paris, is visiting.

관계대명사 종류

who

The teacher who helped me
which

The book which I read
that
사람/사물 (Defining만)
The movie that we watched
whose
소유 (사람/사물)
The student whose phone rang
where

시간

2. 문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

Defining Clauses (제한 관계절)

  • 쉼표 없음 - 문장의 핵심 정보
  • that 사용 가능 - 사람/사물 모두
  • 관계대명사 생략 가능 (목적어일 때)
  • The book (that/which) I read was amazing.
  • The person (who/that) called you is my friend.

Non-Defining Clauses (비제한 관계절)

  • 쉼표로 구분 - 추가 설명
  • that 사용 불가 - who, which만 사용
  • 관계대명사 생략 불가
  • My sister, who lives in Paris, is visiting.
  • This book, which I bought yesterday, is great.

구조와 사용 원칙

  • 관계절은 주어 + 동사 구조를 포함함
  • 선행사(앞에 오는 명사)를 수식함
  • 쉼표 하나로 의미가 완전히 달라질 수 있음
  • 문어체와 구어체 모두에서 중요한 구조

3. 예시 문장 (Example Sentences)

Defining Clauses (제한 관계절)

  • The man who called you is my uncle.
  • The book that I borrowed is interesting.
  • The student whose phone rang was embarrassed.
  • The café where we met is closed now.
  • The day when we arrived was rainy.
  • The teacher who helped me is kind.
  • The movie that we watched was exciting.
  • The girl whose dog barked apologized.
  • The place where I lost my bag was crowded.
  • The time when he called was midnight.
  • The person who answered the phone was friendly.
  • The computer which broke yesterday is fixed now.

Non-Defining Clauses (비제한 관계절)

  • My sister, who lives in Paris, is visiting.
  • This book, which I bought yesterday, is amazing.
  • Mr. Kim, whose son is in my class, is a doctor.
  • The museum, where we took photos, was beautiful.
  • Monday, when we arrived, was very hot.
  • My friend, who helped me, is moving away.
  • The laptop, which broke last week, is fixed now.
  • Jenny, whose dog barked, said sorry.
  • That park, where we played, is near my house.
  • Yesterday, when I saw him, he looked tired.
  • My mother, who called this morning, is worried.
  • Seoul, where I was born, is a beautiful city.

5. 상황별 대화 (Situational Dialogues)

학교 상황
A: Who is the student whose phone rang?
B: It was Tom, who sits next to me.
A: Oh, the boy who wears glasses?
B: Yes, that's him.
여행 상황
A: The café where we met is closed now.
B: Really? That place, which had great coffee, was my favorite.
A: Mine too.
직장 상황
A: The report that you sent was excellent.
B: Thanks. Mr. Lee, who reviewed it, gave good feedback.
A: He's the manager who joined last month, right?
B: Yes.
온라인 상황
A: The website that crashed is working again.
B: Good. The developer, who fixed it, is very skilled.
A: I agree.
가족 상황
A: My cousin, who lives in Canada, is visiting.
B: Is she the one who speaks French?
A: Yes, and she's bringing gifts.
병원 상황
A: The nurse who helped me was very kind.
B: Was it Sarah, who works in Room 3?
A: Yes, that's her.

6. 문장 결합 연습 (Sentence Combining)

두 문장을 관계절로 결합하기

The man called you. He is my uncle.
→ The man who called you is my uncle.
The book is interesting. I borrowed it.
→ The book that I borrowed is interesting.
The student was embarrassed. His phone rang.
→ The student whose phone rang was embarrassed.
The café is closed. We met there.
→ The café where we met is closed.
The day was rainy. We arrived then.
→ The day when we arrived was rainy.
My sister lives in Paris. She is visiting. (추가 정보)
→ My sister, who lives in Paris, is visiting.
This book is amazing. I bought it yesterday. (추가 정보)
→ This book, which I bought yesterday, is amazing.
Mr. Kim is a doctor. His son is in my class. (추가 정보)
→ Mr. Kim, whose son is in my class, is a doctor.
The museum was beautiful. We took photos there. (추가 정보)
→ The museum, where we took photos, was beautiful.
Monday was hot. We arrived then. (추가 정보)
→ Monday, when we arrived, was very hot.

7. 실생활 사용 (Real-Life Usage)

이메일 안내

The client, who requested changes, will review the file tomorrow. The report that you sent looks good.

자기소개

I'm working with a team that develops educational tools. My colleague, who speaks Korean, helps with localization.

업무 회의

The document that outlines the plan is ready. Mr. Park, whose team leads the project, will present it.

여행 후기

The city where we stayed was peaceful. My friend, who booked the hotel, found a great deal.

건강 상담

The nurse who assisted me was very kind. Dr. Lee, whose clinic is nearby, gave helpful advice.

11. 레벨업 팁 (Level-Up Tips)

관계절 마스터 팁

  • Defining Clause: 문장의 핵심 정보 → 쉼표 없음
  • Non-Defining Clause: 추가 설명 → 쉼표 있음
  • "that"은 Defining에서만 사용
  • "which"은 Non-Defining에서 사용
  • "whose"는 소유 관계를 표현
  • "where / when"은 장소 / 시간 정보 연결
  • 관계절은 주어 + 동사를 포함해야 함
  • 쉼표 하나로 의미가 완전히 달라질 수 있음
  • 관계절은 글쓰기와 말하기 모두에서 중요
