1. 기본 개념 (Basic Concept)

PRESENTSimple Present Tense

현재 일반 시제: 현재의 **사실, 습관, 반복되는 행동**을 표현
주어에 따라 동사 형태가 달라짐 (**3인칭 단수** → 동사 + **s/es**)
긍정문, 부정문, 의문문 모두 **do/does**라는 조동사를 사용

I play soccer.

She plays soccer. (3인칭 단수)

Do you play soccer? (조동사 do 사용)

2. 문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

  • 긍정문: 주어 + 동사 (3인칭 단수 → 동사 + s/es)
  • 부정문: 주어 + **do/does + not** + **동사원형**
  • 의문문: **Do/Does** + 주어 + **동사원형**?
  • **I/You/We/They → 동사원형** (예: play, eat, like)
  • **He/She/It → 동사 + s/es** (예: plays, eats, likes)
  • **부정문 예:** She does not play soccer. (does를 썼으므로 play는 원형)
  • **의문문 예:** Does she play soccer? (Does를 썼으므로 play는 원형)
  • **자주 쓰이는 동사:** like, want, eat, go, have, live, work
  • **빈도 부사:** always, usually, often, sometimes, never (습관을 강조)

3. 긍정문 예시 (Positive Examples)

  • I eat breakfast at 7.
  • You go to school every day.
  • We live in Seoul.
  • They play soccer on weekends.
  • He likes pizza. (3인칭 단수)
  • She works at a hospital. (3인칭 단수)
  • It rains a lot in spring. (3인칭 단수)
  • He has a dog. (have의 3인칭 단수)
  • She wants a new phone.
  • My brother studies English. (3인칭 단수)

4. 의문문 예시 (Question Examples)

  • Do you eat breakfast? (Do 사용)
  • Do they play soccer?
  • Does he like pizza? (Does 사용, 동사원형 like)
  • Does she work here?
  • Do we have class today?
  • Does it rain in summer?
  • Do you want coffee?
  • Does your friend study English?
  • Do they live in Korea?
  • Does she have a dog?

5. 상황별 대화 흐름 (Situational Dialogues)

학교 상황
A: Do you study English?
B: Yes, I study every day.
A: Good job!

여행 상황
A: Does she like Paris?
B: Yes, she loves it.
A: Me too.

직장 상황
A: Does he work here?
B: No, he works at the bank.
A: I see.

온라인 상황
A: Do you use this app?
B: Yes, I use it every day.
A: It’s helpful.

가족 상황
A: Does your brother play soccer?
B: Yes, he plays every weekend.
A: Cool!

병원 상황
A: Does she feel better?
B: Yes, she feels fine now.
A: That’s good.

6. 문장 결합 연습 (Sentence Combining)

  • I eat breakfast. She eat breakfast. → I eat breakfast. She eats breakfast.
  • They play soccer. He play soccer. → They play soccer. He plays soccer.
  • We go to school. She go to school. → We go to school. She goes to school.
  • I like pizza. He like pizza. → I like pizza. He likes pizza.
  • You have a dog. She have a dog. → You have a dog. She has a dog.
  • I study English. My brother study English. → I study English. My brother studies English.
  • We live in Seoul. It live in Seoul. → We live in Seoul. It lives in Seoul.
  • I want coffee. She want coffee. → I want coffee. She wants coffee.
  • They work here. He work here. → They work here. He works here.
  • You eat lunch. She eat lunch. → You eat lunch. She eats lunch.

7. 실생활 사용 예시 (Real-Life Usage)

  • 학교 상황: My teacher **gives** homework every day. (습관/반복)
  • 직장 상황: He **works** from 9 to 5. (사실/일상)
  • 가족 상황: My mom **cooks** dinner at 6. (습관/반복)
  • 온라인 상황: I **check** my email every morning. (습관/반복)
  • 병원 상황: The nurse **helps** patients. (사실/일반적인 역할)
  • 여행 상황: We **visit** new places every summer. (습관/반복)
  • 회의 상황: Our team **starts** meetings on time. (사실/일반적인 사실)

8. 자주 쓰이는 패턴 (Common Patterns)

  • **I/You/We/They + 동사원형:** I **eat**, They **play** (단순 현재)
  • **He/She/It + 동사 + s/es:** She **likes**, He **goes** (3인칭 단수)
  • **Do + 주어 + 동사원형:** Do you **like** coffee? (질문)
  • **Does + 주어 + 동사원형:** Does she **work** here? (질문, 3인칭 단수)
  • **주어 + do/does not + 동사원형:** He **does not like** pizza. (부정)
  • **빈도 부사 + 동사:** I **always eat** breakfast. (습관 강조)
  • **동사 + 목적어:** She **has** a dog. (소유/사실)
  • **동사 + 장소/시간 표현:** They **live** in Seoul. / We **start** at 9.
  • **have**의 3인칭 단수 변화: She **has** a car.
  • **do/does** 사용 시: **동사원형**만 쓴다. (Does he **like**? ❌ Does he likes?)

9. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

  • ❌ She eat breakfast. → ✅ She **eats** breakfast. (3인칭 단수 동사 변화 누락)
  • ❌ He go to school. → ✅ He **goes** to school. (go, do 등은 -es로 끝남)
  • ❌ Does she likes pizza? → ✅ Does she **like** pizza? (Do/Does 사용 후 동사원형 사용)
  • ❌ She have a dog. → ✅ She **has** a dog. (have의 3인칭 단수 형태는 has)
  • ❌ I not work here. → ✅ I **do not work** here. (일반동사 부정문에는 do/does not 필수)

10. 레벨업 팁 (Level-Up Tips)

  • **3인칭 단수**일 때 동사에 **s/es** 붙이는 규칙을 자동화하세요. (He/She/It = S)
  • **부정문/의문문**에서는 **do/does**가 동사를 대신하여 시제를 표현하므로, 본동사는 **반드시 원형**을 사용해야 합니다.
  • **불규칙 3인칭 단수:** **have → has** (가장 자주 틀리는 형태입니다.)
  • 빈도 부사 (always, usually, often, never)를 사용하여 **습관**을 더 명확하게 표현하는 연습을 하세요. (예: He **never eats** fish.)
  • Be 동사 (am/is/are)와 일반 동사를 **혼동하지 않도록** 주의하세요. (❌ I am go home. / ✅ I go home. / ✅ I am happy.)

11. 연습문제 (Practice Exercises)

객관식

  1. Choose the correct verb form:
    My father ___ to work every day.
    A. go
    B. goes
    C. is going
  2. Which is the correct question form?
    They like watching movies. (Question)
    A. Do they like watching movies?
    B. Does they like watching movies?
    C. Are they like watching movies?
  3. Fill in the blank for a negative sentence:
    It ___ rain much in the desert.
    A. is not
    B. do not
    C. does not

1. B. My father (3인칭 단수)는 He에 해당하므로 **goes**가 정답입니다.

2. A. They (복수)는 **Do**를 사용하며, 동사는 원형인 **like**를 씁니다.

3. C. It (3인칭 단수)의 부정문은 **does not**입니다. (일반동사 rain의 부정)

12. 자가진단 체크리스트 (Self-Check)

학습 완료 체크

0%
  • I can use verbs correctly for I, You, We, and They.
  • I can change the verb correctly for He, She, and It (add -s/es).
  • I can form negative sentences using "do not" and "does not."
  • I can form questions using "Do" and "Does" with the base form of the verb.
  • I can correct common mistakes, especially with "have" and "do/does" usage.

자가진단 문항

  1. Can you describe your morning routine using 3 simple present sentences?
  2. Can you ask a question about your friend’s job using "Does she..."?
  3. Can you write a short negative sentence about something you don't eat?
  4. Can you explain why "He go" is wrong and what the correction is?
  5. Can you use the verb "watch" correctly in a sentence about a singular subject (e.g., a child)?

다음 단계 학습 가이드

  • Review the **-s/-es** ending rules for verbs ending in -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, and -o (e.g., watch → watches, go → goes).
  • Practice creating short dialogues that alternate between **Do/Does questions** and **Yes/No answers**.
  • Start making the connection to **adverbs of frequency** (always, sometimes) to make your present tense descriptions richer.
  • Record yourself describing the habits of your family members to build fluency with the 3rd person singular forms.
