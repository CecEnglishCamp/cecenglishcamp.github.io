G34: Question Tags

A2 Grammar Lesson – 부가의문문

기본 개념 (Concept)

Question Tags는 문장 끝에 짧은 질문을 붙여 상대방의 동의나 확인을 구하는 표현입니다. 영어 대화에서 매우 자주 사용되며, 상대방과의 상호작용을 자연스럽게 유도합니다.

긍정문 + 부정 태그

You're tired, aren't you?

She likes music, doesn't she?

부정문 + 긍정 태그

You don't like coffee, do you?

She isn't coming, is she?

억양에 따른 의미 차이:

You're coming, aren't you? ↗ (진짜 궁금할 때)

You're coming, aren't you? ↘ (확신하며 확인할 때)

예시:

It's a beautiful day, isn't it? (아름다운 날이네요, 그렇지 않아요?)
You don't speak German, do you? (독일어 못 하시죠, 그렇죠?)
We should leave now, shouldn't we? (이제 가야 하지 않을까요?)

문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

기본 규칙

  • 주절의 시제와 조동사에 따라 태그가 결정됨
  • 조동사가 있으면 그 조동사를 태그에 사용
  • 조동사가 없으면 do/does/did 사용
  • 긍정문 뒤에는 부정 태그
  • 부정문 뒤에는 긍정 태그

Be 동사와 태그

  • You are happy, aren't you?
  • She is a teacher, isn't she?
  • They were late, weren't they?
  • He was here, wasn't he?
  • 특별한 경우: I'm late, aren't I? (amn't는 사용하지 않음)

조동사와 태그

  • You can swim, can't you?
  • She will come, won't she?
  • We should go, shouldn't we?
  • He must be tired, mustn't he?
  • They could help, couldn't they?

일반동사와 태그

  • You like coffee, don't you?
  • She speaks French, doesn't she?
  • He played soccer, didn't he?
  • They work here, don't they?
  • We met before, didn't we?

주어 변화 규칙

  • I → you: I'm late, aren't I? (예외)
  • You → you: You're tired, aren't you?
  • He → he: He's busy, isn't he?
  • She → she: She works here, doesn't she?
  • They → they: They like music, don't they?

긍정문 + 부정 태그 예시 (Positive + Negative Tag)

Be 동사

  • You're tired, aren't you? (피곤하시죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • She is a teacher, isn't she? (선생님이시죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • We are friends, aren't we? (우리 친구죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • He was at the party, wasn't he? (파티에 있었죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • They were late, weren't they? (늦었었죠, 그렇지 않나요?)

조동사

  • You can swim, can't you? (수영할 수 있죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • She will help us, won't she? (도와줄 거죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • We should go now, shouldn't we? (이제 가야 하죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • He must be tired, mustn't he? (피곤할 거죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • They could come, couldn't they? (올 수 있죠, 그렇지 않나요?)

일반동사

  • You like music, don't you? (음악 좋아하죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • She speaks English, doesn't she? (영어 하죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • We met before, didn't we? (전에 만났었죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • He plays soccer, doesn't he? (축구 하죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • They work here, don't they? (여기서 일하죠, 그렇지 않나요?)

특별한 경우

  • I'm late, aren't I? (제가 늦었죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • I'm right, aren't I? (제가 맞죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
  • I'm in trouble, aren't I? (제가 곤란한 상황이죠, 그렇지 않나요?)

부정문 + 긍정 태그 예시 (Negative + Positive Tag)

Be 동사

  • You aren't busy, are you? (바쁘지 않으시죠, 그렇죠?)
  • She isn't coming, is she? (오지 않죠, 그렇죠?)
  • We aren't late, are we? (늦지 않았죠, 그렇죠?)
  • He wasn't at school, was he? (학교에 없었죠, 그렇죠?)
  • They weren't invited, were they? (초대받지 않았죠, 그렇죠?)

조동사

  • You can't drive, can you? (운전 못 하죠, 그렇죠?)
  • She won't mind, will she? (신경 안 쓸 거죠, 그렇죠?)
  • We shouldn't wait, should we? (기다리면 안 되죠, 그렇죠?)
  • He mustn't smoke here, must he? (여기서 담배 피우면 안 되죠, 그렇죠?)
  • They couldn't come, could they? (올 수 없었죠, 그렇죠?)

일반동사

  • You don't like coffee, do you? (커피 안 좋아하죠, 그렇죠?)
  • She doesn't speak German, does she? (독일어 못 하죠, 그렇죠?)
  • We didn't meet before, did we? (전에 만난 적 없죠, 그렇죠?)
  • He doesn't play tennis, does he? (테니스 안 하죠, 그렇죠?)
  • They don't work here, do they? (여기서 일 안 하죠, 그렇죠?)

특별한 경우

  • I'm not wrong, am I? (제가 틀리지 않았죠, 그렇죠?)
  • I'm not late, am I? (제가 늦지 않았죠, 그렇죠?)
  • I'm not bothering you, am I? (방해하지 않죠, 그렇죠?)

의문문 예시 (Question Tag Examples)

  • A: You're coming to the party, aren't you? (파티에 오시죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
    B: Yes, I'll be there at 8. (네, 8시에 갈게요)
  • A: She doesn't eat meat, does she? (고기 안 먹죠, 그렇죠?)
    B: No, she's vegetarian. (네, 채식주의자예요)
  • A: We were supposed to meet at 3, weren't we? (3시에 만나기로 했죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
    B: Yes, but I'm running late. (네, 근데 늦고 있어요)
  • A: He isn't very talkative, is he? (말이 별로 없죠, 그렇죠?)
    B: No, he's quite shy. (네, 꽤 수줍어해요)
  • A: You can help me with this, can't you? (이거 도와주실 수 있죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
    B: Of course, what do you need? (물론이죠, 뭐가 필요하세요?)
  • A: They won't mind if we're a bit late, will they? (조금 늦어도 신경 안 쓸 거죠, 그렇죠?)
    B: I don't think so. (그럴 것 같지 않아요)
  • A: I'm not interrupting anything, am I? (방해하지 않죠, 그렇죠?)
    B: Not at all, come in. (전혀 아니에요, 들어오세요)
  • A: She should apologize, shouldn't she? (사과해야 하지 않을까요, 그렇지 않나요?)
    B: I think you're right. (그렇게 생각해요)
  • A: We don't need to buy tickets in advance, do we? (미리 표를 살 필요 없죠, 그렇죠?)
    B: Actually, we do. It's a popular show. (사실은 필요해요. 인기 있는 공연이거든요)
  • A: He was here earlier, wasn't he? (아까 여기 있었죠, 그렇지 않나요?)
    B: Yes, about an hour ago. (네, 한 시간 전쯤에요)

상황별 대화 흐름 (Situational Dialogues)

학교 상황 (School Situation)
A: You finished your homework, didn't you?
B: Yes, I did it last night.
A: The test is tomorrow, isn't it?
B: I think so. You studied for it, didn't you?
A: Not yet. I should start now, shouldn't I?
B: Definitely. You don't want to fail, do you?

직장 상황 (Work Situation)
A: You sent the email to the client, didn't you?
B: Yes, this morning.
A: The meeting starts at 2, doesn't it?
B: That's right. You have the presentation ready, don't you?
A: Almost finished. It won't take long, will it?
B: No, probably just an hour.

여행 계획 (Travel Planning)
A: We're taking the 10 AM train, aren't we?
B: Yes, from Central Station.
A: The station isn't far from here, is it?
B: No, just 15 minutes by bus. You have your ticket, don't you?
A: Yes, in my bag. We don't need to arrive early, do we?
B: I'd recommend getting there 30 minutes before.

레스토랑 상황 (Restaurant Situation)
A: This restaurant is quite popular, isn't it?
B: Yes, we were lucky to get a table.
A: You don't eat seafood, do you?
B: Actually, I do. You're thinking of my sister, aren't you?
A: Oh yes, you're right. She's the vegetarian, isn't she?
B: Exactly. Should we order some wine, shouldn't we?

날씨 대화 (Weather Conversation)
A: It's quite cold today, isn't it?
B: Yes, much colder than yesterday.
A: You didn't bring a jacket, did you?
B: No, I forgot. It wasn't supposed to be this cold, was it?
A: The weather forecast is often wrong, isn't it?
B: Unfortunately, yes.

문장 결합 연습 (Sentence Combining)

문장에 적절한 question tag를 추가하는 연습입니다:

You are tired. → You are tired, aren't you?
She doesn't eat meat. → She doesn't eat meat, does she?
  • We met before. → We met before, didn't we?
  • He isn't busy. → He isn't busy, is he?
  • I'm late. → I'm late, aren't I?
  • They play soccer. → They play soccer, don't they?
  • You weren't at school. → You weren't at school, were you?
  • She can swim. → She can swim, can't she?
  • We shouldn't wait. → We shouldn't wait, should we?
  • He will help us. → He will help us, won't he?
  • You don't like spicy food. → You don't like spicy food, do you?
  • She was here yesterday. → She was here yesterday, wasn't she?
  • They must be tired. → They must be tired, mustn't they?
  • You haven't seen him. → You haven't seen him, have you?

실생활 사용 예시 (Real-Life Usage)

이메일 확인

You received the file I sent, didn't you? Please let me know if you have any questions.

회의 중 확인

We're all in agreement about the new proposal, aren't we? I'll proceed with the implementation.

전화 통화

She didn't call back about the interview, did she? I was expecting to hear from her today.

일상 대화

It's a beautiful day for a picnic, isn't it? We should go to the park.

수업 중 확인

You all understood the grammar point, didn't you? Feel free to ask if anything is unclear.

자주 쓰이는 표현 패턴 (Common Patterns)

  • Be동사 + 태그: He's here, isn't he?
  • 조동사 + 태그: She can help, can't she?
  • Do/does/did + 태그: You like coffee, don't you?
  • 부정문 + 긍정 태그: They don't know, do they?
  • I'm + aren't I: I'm late, aren't I?
  • Will + won't: He will come, won't he?
  • Should + shouldn't: We should leave, shouldn't we?
  • Was/were + wasn't/weren't: You
