지문 읽기 READING

In Luggnagg, Gulliver hears about the Struldbruggs — people who are born immortal

He thinks they must be the happiest people alive

But the truth is horrifying

The Struldbruggs grow old but cannot die

They lose their memories

They lose their health

Immortality without eternal youth is a curse.

The Struldbruggs episode is Swift's most direct philosophical argument

Gulliver initially romanticizes immortality

Swift demolishes this fantasy: immortality without youth is not life extended but death prolonged

The proper response to death is not to wish for its removal but to live well within it.

문법 포인트 GRAMMAR

used to / would — 과거 습관 (G41)
  • "The girls used to perform plays in the attic." — used to 과거 습관
  • "Jo would write for hours every night." — would 과거 반복
  • "They used to be poor but were always happy." — 상태 vs 동작

토론 주제 DEBATE

"If it were possible, immortality would be a curse rather than a blessing."
가능하다면, 불멸은 축복이 아닌 저주일 것이다.
✅ 찬성
  • Jo March proves women can succeed as writers
  • Education gives women dignity and independence
  • Limiting women to marriage wastes their talents
❌ 반대
  • Society had no structure for working women at the time
  • Marriage provided financial security
  • Cultural norms were too strong to be easily changed
