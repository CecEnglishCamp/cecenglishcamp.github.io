2 Reading · 오늘의 본문
We · Yevgeny Zamyatin · 1924
Ernest Everhard, a socialist revolutionary, saw the suffering of the workers in the mills and factories. He knew kindness alone could not break the Iron Heel's grip. He began to speak in secret meetings, his words a spark in the dark. He did not just talk of individual charity; he spoke of collective power, of workers uniting as one fist. He argued that their poverty was not a personal failure, but a system designed to crush them. Slowly, a network of resistance grew. People who had felt alone discovered strength in their shared struggle. They realized that their hope lay not in pleading with their oppressors, but in standing together against the entire structure of exploitation.
B2 · 128 words avg 25.6 w/s
Jack London's *The Iron Heel* meticulously dismantles the myth of individual salvation within a corrupt oligarchy. The novel's core argument, channeled through Ernest Everhard, posits that moral rectitude is rendered impotent against institutionalized exploitation. Today's focus on resistance and unity emerges as the logical, indispensable conclusion to this premise. The narrative illustrates that isolated acts of conscience are swiftly crushed by the coordinated might of the 'Oligarchy'. True agency, therefore, is not found in solitary heroism but forged through collective political awakening. The discovery of solidarity transforms the working class from a disparate mass of victims into a nascent political entity. This unity is portrayed not as a sentimental ideal, but as a strategic necessity—the only structural counterweight capable of challenging a system engineered to perpetuate inequality through division and fear. What makes The Iron Heel endure as a work of literature is precisely this refusal to offer easy consolation — it demands that readers sit with the discomfort of a world that does not resolve neatly, and find their own position within it.

5 Grammar · 문법 포인트
which demand / structural A and B
Sinclair's novel argues that individual effort and personal virtue are never sufficient, and that poverty and exploitation are structural problems ___ demand structural solutions through genuine collective political engagement.
'which'는 사물이나 개념을 받는 관계대명사입니다. 'structural problems(구조적 문제들)'은 사물/개념이므로 'which'를 사용합니다. 'who'는 사람, 'where'는 장소, 'when'은 시간을 받을 때 사용합니다.

샘플 에세이
While personal responsibility is admirable, *The Iron Heel* powerfully demonstrates its limits against systemic injustice. Jack London argues that exploitation is embedded in the economic structure itself. Therefore, morally good individuals, acting alone, are ineffective. The novel contends that only organized, collective action—a structural solution—can dismantle a corrupt system. Change requires shifting focus from individual charity to transforming the political and economic rules that enable inequality. Everhard's mission is to build this collective power.
