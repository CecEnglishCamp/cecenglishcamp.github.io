지문 읽기 READING

The relationship between economic power and political influence becomes increasingly apparent in modern democratic systems, where financial resources often determine electoral outcomes and policy directions. Without substantial economic reforms, political equality remains an elusive ideal that fails to materialize in practical governance. Campaign financing systems demonstrate how economic advantages translate directly into political access and legislative influence. When corporate interests dominate political contributions, regulatory frameworks become systematically biased toward protecting capital rather than citizens. This dynamic creates a feedback loop where economic power reinforces political control, which in turn safeguards economic privileges. Consequently, formal democratic institutions may persist while substantive democratic practice erodes beneath the surface of procedural legitimacy.

The intricate nexus between economic concentration and political autonomy constitutes a fundamental challenge to democratic theory and practice, particularly when examining the mechanisms through which capital accumulation translates into systemic political advantage. Without comprehensive redistribution mechanisms or stringent campaign finance regulations, electoral politics inevitably becomes skewed toward representing propertied interests rather than popular will. The transformation of economic supremacy into political hegemony occurs through multiple channels: lobbying expenditures that shape legislative agendas, media ownership patterns that frame public discourse, and revolving door employment between regulatory agencies and regulated industries. When wealth becomes sufficiently concentrated within a narrow stratum of society, political institutions gradually become responsive primarily to that stratum's preferences, regardless of formal electoral procedures. This structural reality suggests that genuine political democracy cannot emerge or endure without corresponding economic democracy, as asymmetrical resource distribution systematically distorts both representation and policy outcomes.

문법 GRAMMAR

G30이번 주 문법 포인트
become + adjective / without + noun
become + 형용사 / without + 명사

❌ 정치적 권리와 자유는 경제적 조건과 독립적으로 보호될 수 있으며, 강력한 법적·제도적 장치로 경제적 영향력을 견제할 수 있다
