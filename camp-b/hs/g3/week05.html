지문 읽기
📡 텔레스크린이 당신을 감시합니다. B1으로 읽어드려요. C1은 수능 실전 수준이에요.

Orwell explains doublethink through Winston's experiences. Doublethink means holding two contradictory beliefs simultaneously and accepting both as true.

The most powerful example is the Party's slogan: 2+2=5. The more Winston resists this idea, the more dangerous his situation becomes. The Party does not need Winston to merely pretend — it needs him to truly believe.

Newspeak is the Party's official language. It is being reduced year by year until there are no words left for independent thought. The dictionary is shrinking, not growing. By 2050, Oldspeak will be gone entirely.

Such is the power of language control that a thought unexpressible in words becomes literally unthinkable. The Party does not burn books — it makes books unnecessary by destroying the language needed to understand them.

Doublethink is Orwell's most original political concept and arguably his most prophetic. It describes the psychological mechanism by which authoritarian systems maintain their hold not through coercion but through the corruption of the cognitive faculty itself. The genius of doublethink is that it does not require the subject to lie — it requires them to genuinely not know that they are lying.

Newspeak operationalises doublethink linguistically. By eliminating the vocabulary of dissent, it does not merely censor thought — it prevents its formation. The difference between censorship and Newspeak is the difference between locking a door and removing the concept of a door from human consciousness.

Orwell's warning to his own era — and to ours — is not that governments will ban certain books. It is that governments will reshape the language in which thought occurs, until the thoughts that most need thinking become impossible to think.

오웰은 윈스턴의 경험을 통해 이중사고를 설명해요. 이중사고는 두 가지 모순된 믿음을 동시에 가지고 둘 다 진실로 받아들이는 것을 의미해요. 뉴스피크는 당의 공식 언어예요. 해마다 줄어들어서 독립적 사고를 위한 단어가 없어져요. 말로 표현할 수 없는 생각은 문자 그대로 생각할 수 없게 돼요.

29번 유형
Orwell's concept of Newspeak is based on the premise that language does not merely express thought but shapes it. By systematically reducing vocabulary, the Party ensures that certain ideas become literally impossible to conceive. This represents a form of control more absolute than censorship because it does not suppress thought after it has occurred — it prevents By reducing the vocabulary available to citizens through Newspeak, the Party ensures that certain concepts become literally unthinkable. A person who lacks the words to express rebellion cannot rebel; a person who cannot conceive of an alternative to the present cannot imagine change. This is why Winston's act of writing in his diary is so significant: it is not merely an act of courage but an act of          — a refusal to allow his inner life to be colonised by the Party's language.
