지문 읽기
📡 텔레스크린이 당신을 감시합니다. B1으로 읽어드려요. C1은 수능 실전 수준이에요.

Winston and Julia are arrested. They are taken to the Ministry of Love — the torture centre of Oceania. Winston has known all along that this moment would come.

O'Brien oversees Winston's torture. He explains the Party's true purpose: not wealth, not happiness, but power — pure, unlimited power over human beings. Power is not a means; it is the end.

Finally, Winston is taken to Room 101. Whatever a person fears most is in Room 101. For Winston, it is rats. When a cage of rats is placed near his face, he screams one thing:

"Do it to Julia! Not me! Julia!" So complete is his betrayal that he cannot take it back. He has broken the one promise that mattered. The Party has won.

Room 101 is the novel's most psychologically precise moment. Orwell understood that torture's ultimate goal is not information but transformation. What the Party wants from Winston is not his confession but his love — his genuine, unforced acceptance of Big Brother as good.

The rat cage achieves this by targeting the deepest, most irrational layer of Winston's psyche — the layer that exists below ideology, below love, below any conscious value. So deeply does it penetrate that Winston's betrayal of Julia is not strategic but automatic: a pure reflex of survival.

This is Orwell's most pessimistic claim: that there is a point in every person beyond which no loyalty, no love, no principle can hold. Room 101 is not a place — it is the argument that the self, under sufficient pressure, will always choose its own continuation over everything else.

윈스턴과 줄리아가 체포돼요. 오브라이언이 고문을 감독해요: 당의 진정한 목적은 권력이에요 — 순수하고 무제한적인 권력이에요. 마침내 윈스턴이 101호실로 끌려가요. 쥐 우리가 그의 얼굴 가까이 놓이자 그는 소리쳐요: "줄리아에게 해! 나한테 하지 마!" 그의 배신은 너무 완전해서 되돌릴 수 없어요.

Orwell uses this scene to make his most extreme claim: that the self, under sufficient pressure, will always prioritise its own survival.
