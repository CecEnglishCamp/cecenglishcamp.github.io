지문 읽기
📡 텔레스크린이 당신을 감시합니다. B1으로 읽어드려요. C1은 수능 실전 수준이에요.

Winston Smith lives in Oceania, a totalitarian state ruled by a mysterious figure known as Big Brother. The Party controls everything — history, language, thought itself. Telescreens watch every citizen at every moment.

Winston works at the Ministry of Truth, where his job is to rewrite old newspaper articles to match the Party's current version of history. If the Party says it never happened, it never happened. The past is whatever the Party decides it to be.

Winston secretly hates the Party. He buys an illegal diary and begins to write. Writing "DOWN WITH BIG BROTHER" on the page, he commits thoughtcrime — a crime so serious that it is punishable by death.

He knows that the Thought Police will find him eventually. But he writes anyway. His act of writing is his first act of rebellion — a small insistence that his own experience is real, that the past cannot be entirely erased.

Orwell establishes Oceania as a system designed not merely to control behaviour but to make independent thought structurally impossible. The telescreens, the Thought Police, the perpetual war — these are not the primary instruments of control. The primary instrument is language itself, reduced through Newspeak to a vocabulary so limited that certain thoughts become literally unthinkable.

Winston's diary is therefore not simply an act of courage — it is an act of ontological resistance. By writing down his experience in his own words, he asserts the existence of a private reality that the Party cannot reach. The Ministry of Truth, where he works, is the institution responsible for destroying this private reality at scale.

The novel's central question is not whether Winston can escape — Orwell makes clear from the beginning that he cannot. The question is whether the truth can survive at all in a system designed to abolish it. Winston's diary is Orwell's answer: even under totalitarianism, the impulse to record, to remember, to insist on one's own experience — that impulse is the last territory that power cannot fully conquer.

윈스턴 스미스는 오세아니아에 살아요. 빅 브라더로 알려진 신비로운 인물이 통치하는 전체주의 국가예요. 당은 모든 것을 통제해요 — 역사, 언어, 사고 그 자체까지요. 텔레스크린이 매 순간 모든 시민을 감시해요.

윈스턴은 진리부에서 일해요. 그곳에서 그의 임무는 당의 현재 버전의 역사에 맞게 오래된 신문 기사를 다시 쓰는 거예요. 그는 불법으로 일기를 구입하고 쓰기 시작해요. "빅 브라더 타도"라고 쓰며 그는 사상죄를 저질러요 — 사형에 처할 수 있는 범죄예요.

그는 사상경찰이 결국 그를 찾을 것임을 알아요. 하지만 그래도 써요. 그의 쓰기 행위는 첫 번째 반란 행위예요.

핵심 표현 분석 — Doublethink
이번 주 KEY EXPRESSION
"War is Peace. Freedom is Slavery. Ignorance is Strength."
War is Peace = 전쟁은 평화다 → 외부 전쟁이 내부 통합을 만든다
Freedom is Slavery = 자유는 노예다 → 개인 자유는 오히려 고립과 취약함
Ignorance is Strength = 무지는 힘이다 → 모르면 저항할 수 없다
💡 이 슬로건들이 수능에 나오는 이유:
모순(contradiction) → 역설(paradox) → 역설이 진실이 되는 구조
→ 빈칸 추론, 요약문 완성, 주제 파악 문제 단골 소재
🔍
문장 구조 분석 → 재생산
구조 분석은 해석이 아니라 이해 → 추출 → 재생산이 목표예요.
STEP 1 — 분석 (Analyse)
"Not until he wrote the words did Winston understand the weight of what he had done."
도치 구문
Not until + 절 → 주동 도치
~하고 나서야 비로소 이해했다
부정 부사구
Not until he wrote the words
그 단어들을 쓸 때까지는 아니었다
도치된 동사
did Winston understand
일반: Winston understood
도치: did Winston understand
목적어 (과거완료)
the weight of what he had done
what절 = 명사절 (한 일의 무게)
💡 수능 어법 최빈출: "Not until + 절/구 → did/could/would + 주어 + 동사원형"
"Not until the war ended did people understand the cost."
STEP 2 — 구조 추출 (Extract)
Not until + [주어 + 동사], + did/could/would + [주어] + [동사원형]
핵심: Not until 절 → 반드시 주동 도치
→ 어떤 사건이 일어난 후에야 비로소 깨달음/변화가 생길 때 사용
STEP 3 — 재생산 (Reproduce)
같은 구조로 내 문장을 만들어보세요.
예시: "Not until the telescreen went dark did Winston dare to open his diary."

수능 유형 — 빈칸 추론
29번 유형
In Orwell's 1984, the Party's most powerful tool of control is not the telescreen or the Thought Police — it is language. By reducing the vocabulary available to citizens through Newspeak, the Party ensures that certain concepts become literally unthinkable. A person who lacks the words to express rebellion cannot rebel; a person who cannot conceive of an alternative to the present cannot imagine change. This is why Winston's act of writing in his diary is so significant: it is not merely an act of courage but an act of          — a refusal to allow his inner life to be colonised by the Party's language.

* colonise: 식민화하다  ** conceive of: ~을 상상하다
정답 ③ — linguistic self-determination and cognitive independence

핵심 구조: "not merely courage but ___"
→ 빈칸 = 용기를 넘어선 더 깊은 의미
"inner life colonised by the Party's language" → 언어적 저항
→ ③ "linguistic self-determination and cognitive independence"
(언어적 자기결정과 인지적 독립)
