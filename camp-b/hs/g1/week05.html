지문 읽기
🔊 B1 기준으로 읽어드려요. C1은 수능 실전 수준이에요.

Gatsby had loved Daisy five years earlier, before the war, before he had any money. Now he was rich — and he had built his whole life around the possibility of seeing her again.

He asked Nick to arrange a meeting. On the day itself, Gatsby arrived early and rearranged Nick's flowers obsessively. When Daisy arrived, the first few minutes were painful and awkward.

Then something shifted. Gatsby took them to his mansion and showed Daisy everything — the rooms, the grounds, the view. He pulled out his shirts — dozens of them, in every colour — and threw them into the air.

Daisy buried her face in them and wept. "They're such beautiful shirts," she said. Nick understood that it was not really about the shirts.

The reunion scene is the novel's emotional centre and its most delicate irony. Gatsby has spent five years and an entire fortune constructing the conditions for this moment — and when it arrives, he nearly collapses under the weight of it.

Nick observes that the colossal significance of the green light had now vanished forever. The light was a symbol of possibility; Daisy herself is merely a person.

This is Fitzgerald's central tragedy: the dream is always more vivid than its object. When Gatsby throws his shirts into the air, he is not showing off his wealth — he is showing Daisy what she made possible, what loving her from a distance gave him the drive to become.

개츠비는 5년 전, 전쟁 전, 돈이 없던 시절에 데이지를 사랑했어요. 이제 그는 부자였고 다시 그녀를 만날 가능성을 중심으로 전 생애를 구축했어요.

그는 닉에게 만남을 주선해달라고 했어요. 첫 몇 분은 어색했지만 뭔가가 변했어요. 개츠비가 셔츠를 공중에 던졌고 데이지는 얼굴을 묻고 울었어요. "너무 아름다운 셔츠들이에요." 그것은 셔츠에 관한 것이 아니었어요.

핵심 표현 분석
이번 주 KEY EXPRESSION
"Can't repeat the past? Why of course you can!"
이 문장은 단순한 물리적 동작이 아니에요.
repeat the past = 과거를 반복하다 → 개츠비의 근본적 환상
of course you can = 물론 할 수 있다 → 비극의 씨앗
the past = 과거 → 데이지가 아닌 과거의 가능성
💡 이 표현을 에세이 thesis와 디베이트 opening에서 직접 써보세요.
🔍
문장 구조 분석 → 재생산
구조 분석은 해석이 목적이 아니에요.
이해 → 구조 추출 → 내 문장으로 재생산하는 것이 목표예요.

💡 수능 어법 포인트: "as though + 가정법 과거"
격식: "as though he were trying" / 구어: "as though he was trying"
STEP 2 — 구조 추출 (Extract)
이 문장의 구조를 공식으로 추출하면:
[주어] + was + [과거분사],
as though + [주어] + was + [동사-ing] + [목적어/부사]
이 구조의 핵심: 수동태 상태 + 가정법 비교
→ 의도적 행동이 아닌 욕망의 상태를 표현할 때 사용
STEP 3 — 재생산 (Reproduce)
같은 구조로 내 문장을 만들어보세요.
예시: "Her eyes were fixed on the door,
as though she was waiting for someone who would never come."

29번 유형
When Gatsby finally met Daisy again, his arm stretched toward a green light across the bay. Gatsby had built his entire life around this moment — the parties, the mansion, the wealth — all of it directed toward one small point of light. Nick eventually understood that the green light was not really about Daisy. It was about the belief that the past could be recovered, that time itself could be reversed. This is why Fitzgerald calls Gatsby "great" despite his flaws: his capacity for hope was          in its intensity, if not in its wisdom.

핵심 구조: "great despite his flaws" → 결점에도 불구하고 위대함
