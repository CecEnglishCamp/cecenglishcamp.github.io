지문 읽기
🔊 B1 기준으로 읽어드려요. C1은 수능 실전 수준이에요.

The boys are afraid of a beast — a creature they believe lives on the island. Some claim to have seen it in the dark. Fear spreads quickly.

Ralph calls an assembly to discuss the beast. The meeting quickly becomes chaotic, with everyone speaking at once. Only Simon suggests a different possibility: maybe the beast is not an external creature.

"Maybe," Simon says quietly, "there is a beast, and maybe it's only us." The other boys laugh or fall silent. They do not understand what he means.

Simon alone sees that the beast is not something out there, but something within each of them — the capacity for violence and savagery that civilisation had kept hidden. This makes him the novel's only true prophet.

The beast is Golding's central symbol, and Simon's insight is the key to understanding it. The other boys project their fear outward — they imagine a creature, a monster, something external and identifiable. Simon alone understands that what they fear is a quality of human nature itself.

This distinction matters enormously. If the beast is external, it can be hunted and killed. If the beast is internal — if it is "only us" — then there is no escape from it, no solution except the constant, effortful work of maintaining the social agreements that keep it in check.

What Golding is suggesting is that civilisation is not the natural state of humanity but its achievement — won against the grain, maintained by effort, and lost with terrifying ease. Simon's vision makes him a saint in a world that has no use for saints.

소년들은 야수를 두려워해요 — 섬에 살고 있다고 믿는 생물이에요. 랄프가 야수에 대해 토론하려고 집회를 열어요. 오직 사이먼만이 다른 가능성을 제안해요: "어쩌면 야수는 우리 자신일지도 몰라." 다른 소년들은 웃거나 침묵해요.

사이먼만이 야수가 밖에 있는 것이 아니라 각자 안에 있는 것임을 알아요 — 문명이 숨겨왔던 폭력성과 야만성이에요.
