2

지문 읽기 READING

Boxer is the strongest and most loyal animal on the farm. He always says "I will work harder." He trusts Napoleon completely. When Boxer gets injured and can no longer work, Napoleon sells him to a glue factory. This is the cruelest moment in the novel.

Boxer's fate represents the novel's most devastating critique of totalitarian exploitation. His two mottos — "I will work harder" and "Napoleon is always right" — embody the dangerous combination of physical strength without critical thinking that authoritarian regimes depend upon to sustain themselves.

토론 주제 DEBATE TOPIC

💬 "Blind loyalty to authority is the most dangerous human trait."
권위에 대한 맹목적인 충성심은 가장 위험한 인간적 특성이다.
✅ 찬성 (Agree)
  • Boxer's loyalty enables his own exploitation and death
  • Every authoritarian regime depends on the blind obedience of good people
  • Critical thinking is the only protection against tyranny
❌ 반대 (Disagree)
  • Loyalty and trust are essential foundations of any community
  • Not everyone can be a critical thinker — society needs followers too
  • Boxer's loyalty came from genuine goodness, not weakness
