The War of the Worlds

이야기 읽기
🔊 듣기는 초급 B1 기준으로 읽어드려요.
📌 초급(B1) = 이 학년의 평균 영어 수준. 입문(A2)은 더 쉽게, 중급(B2)은 더 어렵게 조정된 버전이에요.

Strange things happened one night.
A big object fell from the sky.
It landed near London.
People came to look at it.
Nobody knew what it was.
Then something moved inside.
People ran away in fear.
The world was never the same again.

Nobody expected the Martians. The newspapers had reported strange lights on Mars, but scientists said there was nothing to worry about.

Then a great cylinder crashed into the fields near Woking. At first, people only watched from a safe distance. The thing was hot and smoking. Nothing seemed to move.

But something was waiting inside. When the lid unscrewed, a terrible creature emerged — grey, wet, and absolutely unlike anything that had ever lived on Earth.

The crowd screamed and ran. The narrator stood frozen, unable to believe what he was seeing.

No one would have believed, in the last years of the nineteenth century, that human affairs were being watched from the timeless worlds of space. No one could have dreamed that we were being scrutinised, as someone with a microscope scrutinises creatures in a drop of water.

Yet across the gulf of space, minds immeasurably superior to ours regarded this earth with envious eyes, and slowly and surely drew their plans against us.

The first sign was a blinding flash of light on the surface of Mars. Then came the cylinder — enormous, black, and buried deep in the Surrey countryside. Around it stood a crowd of curious humans, none of whom understood that they were looking at the beginning of the end.

The narrator watched the lid begin to unscrew. His neighbour touched his arm. "There's something moving," he said quietly. "Something — or someone."

※ 번역은 초급 B1 기준
아무도 19세기 마지막 해에, 인간의 일들이 우주의 시간을 초월한 세계들에서 관찰되고 있다는 것을 믿지 않았을 것이에요. 우리가 세밀하게 조사받고 있다는 것을 꿈에도 생각하지 못했어요.

하지만 우주의 심연 너머로, 우리보다 헤아릴 수 없을 만큼 뛰어난 지성들이 부러운 눈으로 지구를 바라보며, 천천히 그리고 확실하게 우리를 향한 계획을 세웠어요.

첫 번째 징조는 화성 표면의 눈부신 섬광이었어요. 그리고 실린더가 왔어요 — 거대하고, 검고, 서리 시골 깊은 곳에 박혔어요. 그 주변에는 호기심 많은 인간들이 서 있었어요. 그들 중 누구도 자신들이 종말의 시작을 보고 있다는 것을 이해하지 못했어요.

📌 에세이 3단락 구조 (Paragraph Structure)
① 주제문 (Topic Sentence)
나의 의견을 명확하게 밝혀요.
예) "I think freedom means being able to make my own choices."
② 근거/예시 (Supporting Sentence)
왜 그렇게 생각하는지 예시로 설명해요.
예) "For example, Jim wanted freedom so he could live without fear."
③ 마무리 (Concluding Sentence)
결론을 한 문장으로 마무리해요.
예) "That is why freedom is the most important thing in life."
