"The Martians are unstoppable."
화성인은 막을 수 없어요.

이야기 읽기
🔊 듣기는 초급 B1 기준으로 읽어드려요.
📌 초급(B1) = 이 학년의 평균 수준. 입문(A2)은 더 쉽게, 중급(B2)은 더 어렵게 조정된 버전이에요.

The Martians built big machines.
The machines had three legs.
People called them tripods.
The tripods walked very fast.
Soldiers tried to stop them.
But guns did not work.
A heat ray burned everything.
People ran away in fear.

The next morning, the Martians had assembled their fighting machines. The tripods were protected by an invisible force field. Nothing could get through it.

The army had been sent to stop them. Soldiers fired their rifles. Artillery shells exploded near the tripods. But the weapons were useless.

Then the Heat-Ray appeared — a beam of energy that turned everything to ash. Buildings, soldiers, and horses burned in seconds.

"The Martians are unstoppable," the narrator thought, watching from a hill. Then he turned and ran.

The tripods crossed the Surrey hills with the confidence of creatures who had already won. They were vast, mechanical, and entirely indifferent to the shells bursting around them.

The Heat-Ray did not discriminate. Church spires, artillery positions, civilians — all became ash at the same speed. There was something almost insulting about the efficiency of it.

The narrator watched from high ground as Victorian civilisation — ordered, confident, certain of its own superiority — came apart in a single afternoon.

"The Martians are unstoppable," he thought. And for the first time, he ran without any plan except to survive.

삼각 기계들은 이미 이긴 생명체들의 자신감으로 서리 언덕을 가로질렀어요. 거대하고, 기계적이며, 주변의 포탄에 완전히 무관심했어요.

열광선은 차별하지 않았어요. 교회 첨탑, 포병 진지, 민간인 — 모두 같은 속도로 재가 됐어요.

서술자는 빅토리아 문명이 오후 한나절에 무너지는 것을 지켜봤어요.
"화성인은 막을 수 없어," 그는 생각했어요. 그리고 그냥 달렸어요.
