The house still had food and water.
The curate became very afraid.
He talked too loudly.

The narrator met a man who was sitting alone in a ruined church. The man was a curate — a kind of priest — who had lost all hope.

"God has abandoned us," the curate said, shaking his head. The narrator did not agree, but he sat down next to the man.

Together, they found a house that had not been destroyed. The kitchen that they had entered still had food and water. They decided to hide there and wait.

But the curate who had seemed calm at first began to lose his mind. He talked too loudly and moved too quickly, which was extremely dangerous. Their hiding place was at risk.

The curate was not a bad man. That was the difficult part.

He was simply a man whose world had ended. Everything he had believed about God and goodness and the safety of the world — all of it had been proved wrong in three days. He had no framework left for what was happening.

The narrator understood this. He did not judge the curate. But he also understood that a man who could not control himself in the dark was a man who would get them both killed.

They argued in whispers. They ate in silence. They slept at different times so someone was always watching.

Outside, the Martians moved. Inside, two men watched each other across the growing dark, trying to decide who to trust.

하지만 처음에 침착해 보이던 목사가 이성을 잃기 시작했어요. 너무 크게 말하고 너무 빨리 움직였어요. 이것은 매우 위험했어요.
