지문 읽기 READING

The relentless pursuit of pleasure often begins with small indulgences that gradually grow into obsessions. People convince themselves that momentary happiness justifies any action, ignoring the potential consequences of their choices. This mindset creates a dangerous separation between desire and responsibility.

When individuals prioritize pleasure above all else, they often damage their relationships with others. Friends and family may feel used or neglected as the pleasure-seeker becomes increasingly self-centered. The temporary satisfaction gained from constant indulgence rarely leads to lasting fulfillment.

Eventually, this lifestyle creates an emotional emptiness that no amount of pleasure can fill. The pursuit becomes mechanical, losing its original joy while demanding greater extremes to achieve the same effect. Many discover too late that true contentment requires balance, not excess.

The philosophical underpinnings of hedonism suggest that pleasure constitutes the highest good, yet this premise fails to account for the complex nature of human fulfillment. While immediate gratification provides transient satisfaction, it often undermines long-term wellbeing through neglected responsibilities and damaged social bonds. The relentless pursuit of sensory pleasure, divorced from ethical considerations, ultimately leads to a diminished capacity for genuine connection.

Psychological research indicates that individuals who prioritize pleasure above purpose frequently experience increased anxiety and depression over time. This paradoxical outcome stems from the hedonic treadmill effect, wherein people adapt to pleasure and require ever-greater stimulation to achieve the same emotional response. Consequently, what begins as liberation becomes a form of psychological imprisonment.

True human flourishing appears to emerge from meaningful engagement with the world rather than passive consumption of pleasure. Activities that challenge individuals, foster growth, and contribute to community tend to produce more enduring satisfaction than purely hedonistic pursuits. The most contented lives often balance pleasure with purpose, recognizing that unbridled self-indulgence ultimately diminishes the very joy it seeks to capture.

📐 3단락 구조: 서론 → 본론 → 결론

서론: 흥미로운 사실이나 질문으로 시작 → 주제 소개(쾌락 추구와 그 결과) → 명확한 논제 진술(위의 essay_topic_en 번역 활용). 예시 시작: '인간은 본능적으로 쾌락을 추구하지만, 도리안 그레이의 이야기는...'
