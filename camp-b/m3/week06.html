📌 초급(B1) = 이 학년의 평균 수준. 입문(A2)은 더 쉽게, 중급(B2)은 더 어렵게 조정된 버전이에요.

Three years passed after the falls.
Watson thought Holmes was dead.
Then an old man came to his door.
The old man was Holmes.
He had pretended to be dead.
He was still hunting Moriarty's men.
One dangerous man was still free.
Holmes needed Watson's help one more time.

Three years after the falls at Reichenbach, Watson received an unexpected visitor. The old bookseller who knocked at his door was, in fact, Sherlock Holmes — alive and unchanged.

Watson, whose shock was so great that he nearly fainted, could barely speak. Holmes, smiling calmly, explained that his death had been staged deliberately.

"If the newspapers had not reported my death," he said, "Moriarty's men would never have relaxed." He had spent three years watching from the shadows.

Now one last criminal remained — Colonel Moran, whose skill with a rifle made him extremely dangerous. "If Moran had suspected the trick," Holmes said, "the plan would have failed completely."

Watson had not expected to feel angry. He had expected relief, joy — perhaps even tears. Instead, he found himself almost unable to speak from a kind of fury he could not quite explain.

Holmes waited for it to pass. He had seen Watson angry before. He also knew, better than Watson did, that the anger was only love with nowhere to go.

Holmes explained the plan carefully: a wax model of himself, placed in the window of 221B. Moran, who had been watching for three years, would see it. He would fire. And Holmes would be there, in the empty house across the road, to close the trap.

"It is a dangerous plan," Watson said.

"Yes," said Holmes. "Most good ones are."

Watson picked up his coat. He did not say another word. Holmes had known he would not need to ask twice.
