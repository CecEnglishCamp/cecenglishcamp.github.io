📖 Reading Passage — Grade 5

In Step 9, trainees experience a launch simulation.

A launch simulation is a full practice of the rocket launch sequence.
Everything from the countdown to orbit insertion is practiced in the correct order.

The sequence begins at T-Minus 10.
This means 10 seconds before launch.
"T-Minus" is the term for counting down to launch time.

At T-Minus 10, the crew reports: "T-Minus 10. All systems go for launch!"
At T-Minus 3, the harness is checked one final time.
At T-Minus 1, the engines reach full power.

Then: "3... 2... 1... Ignition! We have liftoff!"

As the rocket climbs, Mission Control monitors every system.
They confirm: "Trajectory nominal. All systems green. GO for orbit!"

When the spacecraft reaches orbit, the crew makes the final report:
"Mission Control, this is CEC Space Camp crew. We have achieved orbit! All crew safe. All systems nominal. Over!"

📖 Reading Passage — Grade 6

A rocket launch is one of the most complex and dangerous events in human spaceflight.
Every second from ignition to orbit insertion must be precisely planned and executed.

The launch countdown begins at T-Minus 10.
Each step in the countdown has a specific purpose:
At T-10, final system confirmations are completed.
At T-5, the main engines begin their ignition sequence.
At T-3, crew performs a final harness verification.
At T-1, engines reach maximum thrust.

At T-0: ignition — the engines fire at full power, and the rocket lifts off the launch pad.

During the first phase of flight, the rocket passes through max-Q — the point of maximum aerodynamic pressure.
The engines throttle down briefly to reduce structural stress, then increase thrust again.

At approximately 8 minutes after liftoff, MECO (Main Engine Cutoff) occurs.
The main engines shut down, and the second stage separates.

The spacecraft then performs an insertion burn to enter the correct orbital path.
Once orbit is confirmed, the crew reports:
"Mission Control, orbit insertion complete. Apogee altitude nominal. All systems operational. We are GO for mission objectives. Over."

The launch simulation teaches trainees that spaceflight is not just about power — it is about precision, timing, and teamwork at every stage.

로켓 발사는 인류 우주비행에서 가장 복잡하고 위험한 사건 중 하나입니다.
점화부터 궤도 진입까지 매 초가 정밀하게 계획되고 실행되어야 합니다.

발사 카운트다운은 T-마이너스 10에서 시작됩니다.
T-10: 최종 시스템 확인. T-5: 주엔진 점화 시퀀스 시작.
T-3: 크루 하네스 최종 검증. T-1: 엔진 최대 추력 도달.

T-0에서 점화 — 엔진이 전력으로 가동되고 로켓이 발사대에서 이륙합니다.

비행 첫 단계에서 로켓은 max-Q를 통과합니다 — 최대 공기역학적 압력 지점입니다.
구조적 스트레스를 줄이기 위해 엔진이 잠시 감속한 후 다시 추력을 높입니다.

이륙 약 8분 후 MECO(주엔진 연소 종료)가 발생합니다.
주엔진이 꺼지고 2단 분리가 이루어집니다.

발사 시뮬레이션이 훈련생들에게 가르치는 것은 우주비행이 단순히 힘에 관한 것이 아니라는 점입니다.
모든 단계에서의 정확성, 타이밍, 팀워크에 관한 것입니다.
