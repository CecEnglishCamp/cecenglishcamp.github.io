📖 Reading Passage — Grade 5

In Step 10, the crew adapts to microgravity.

Microgravity is the condition in space where gravity is very weak.
Objects do not fall. They float.
There is no "up" and no "down."

At first, microgravity feels amazing.
You can float through the spacecraft like a superhero!
But working in microgravity is much harder than it looks.

The crew must follow three safety rules.

Rule 1: Anchor yourself.
Use foot straps or handrails before touching any equipment.
If you are not anchored, every push will send you flying.

Rule 2: Attach everything.
A floating screwdriver can damage equipment.
A floating water bubble can enter electronics and cause a fire.

Rule 3: Move slowly.
In microgravity, there is nothing to stop you once you start moving.
Slow and controlled movement keeps the crew safe.

With practice, astronauts learn to move gracefully through the spacecraft.
Even eating and drinking become special skills in space.

📖 Reading Passage — Grade 6

Microgravity is one of the most profound challenges of long-duration spaceflight.
When humans enter orbit, the body begins to change almost immediately.

On Earth, gravity constantly pulls fluids downward.
In microgravity, this force disappears, causing fluid shifts — body fluids move toward the head.
This can cause facial puffiness, nasal congestion, and a sensation similar to being upside down.

Over time, the body begins to lose bone density and muscle mass because it no longer needs to work against gravity.
Without regular exercise, astronauts can lose up to 1-2% of bone mass per month.

The vestibular system — the inner ear balance organ — also becomes confused.
It cannot determine which way is "up," leading to space motion sickness in many astronauts during the first few days.

To work safely in microgravity, astronauts follow strict protocols:
anchoring the body before any task, securing all loose objects, and making only slow, deliberate movements.

Over time, the brain adapts to the new environment.
Astronauts learn to use the spacecraft's walls, floor, and ceiling equally.
Movement becomes efficient, graceful, and three-dimensional.

Microgravity teaches astronauts — and all of us — that adaptation is possible.
With the right training, humans can thrive even in the most extreme environments.

무중력은 장기 우주비행의 가장 심각한 도전 중 하나입니다.
인간이 궤도에 진입하면 신체는 거의 즉시 변화하기 시작합니다.

지구에서 중력은 지속적으로 체액을 아래로 당깁니다.
무중력에서 이 힘이 사라지면서 체액 이동이 발생합니다 — 체액이 머리 쪽으로 이동합니다.

시간이 지나면서 신체는 골밀도와 근육량을 잃기 시작합니다.
규칙적인 운동 없이는 한 달에 1~2%의 골밀도를 잃을 수 있습니다.

전정 기관 — 내이 균형 기관 — 도 혼란스러워집니다.
어느 쪽이 "위"인지 판단할 수 없어 많은 우주비행사에게 우주 멀미를 일으킵니다.

무중력에서 안전하게 일하기 위해 우주비행사들은 엄격한 프로토콜을 따릅니다.

무중력은 우주비행사들에게 — 그리고 우리 모두에게 — 적응이 가능하다는 것을 가르쳐줍니다.
올바른 훈련으로 인간은 가장 극단적인 환경에서도 번성할 수 있습니다.
