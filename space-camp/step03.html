The trainees learn four important words:

Without these systems, astronauts cannot live in space.

It is a safe place for astronauts.

📖 Reading Passage — Grade 5

In Step 3, trainees learn about spacecraft systems.

A spacecraft is not only a vehicle.
It is a small living and working space for astronauts.
Astronauts need air, water, power, temperature control, and communication.

The life support system helps astronauts breathe.
It provides oxygen and removes bad air.

The power system gives electricity to lights, computers, and equipment.

The communication system connects the spacecraft to Mission Control on Earth.

During the briefing, trainees study a diagram of the spacecraft.
They learn that every system has a job.

If one system has a problem, the crew must report it quickly.

📖 Reading Passage — Grade 6

A spacecraft is one of the most complex machines humans have ever built.
It must travel through space, protect the crew, support life, and communicate with Earth.

The life support system provides oxygen, removes carbon dioxide, controls air pressure, and helps keep the cabin safe for breathing.

The electrical power system supplies energy to computers, lights, scientific tools, heaters, and communication devices.

The communication system allows astronauts to speak with Mission Control.
Clear communication is essential — astronauts must report problems, receive instructions, and confirm procedures.

The thermal control system manages temperature.
Space can be extremely hot in sunlight and extremely cold in shadow.

During the briefing, trainees study diagrams, watch simulations, and practice saying system names.
They learn: a spacecraft is a team of systems, and every system must do its job.

우주선은 인간이 만든 가장 복잡한 기계 중 하나입니다.

생명유지장치는 산소를 제공하고, 이산화탄소를 제거하며, 기압을 조절합니다.

전력 시스템은 컴퓨터, 조명, 과학 장비에 에너지를 공급합니다.

통신 시스템은 우주비행사가 지구 관제센터와 대화할 수 있게 해줍니다.

열 제어 시스템은 온도를 관리합니다. 우주는 햇빛에서 매우 뜨겁고 그림자에서 매우 차갑습니다.

우주선은 하나의 기계가 아닙니다. 여러 시스템이 모인 팀입니다.
