“Uh… ten o’clock… but not too early… maybe after lunch… but before dinner… small time?”

✅ A2 Level

Young-ja calls a hospital.

She says, “I want to make an appointment.”

The nurse asks,

“What time is good for you?”

Young-ja is nervous.

She tries to say the time.

“Ten o’clock… not too early… maybe after lunch… small time?”

The nurse laughs and says,

“So, two o’clock in the afternoon?”

Young-ja says,

“Yes! Two o’clock! Perfect small time!”

✅ B1 Level

Young-ja wanted to see a doctor, so she called the hospital.

She tried to speak in English.

“I’d like to make an appointment,” she said carefully.

The nurse replied kindly,

“Of course. What time would you like?”

Suddenly, Young-ja panicked.

She wasn’t sure how to say the time clearly.

“Ten o’clock… not too early… maybe after lunch… before dinner… a small time?” she said.

The nurse smiled and helped her.

“Do you mean two o’clock in the afternoon?”

Relieved, Young-ja answered,

“Yes, two o’clock! Perfect small time!”

After the call, she shook her head.

“Why did I say small time?” she laughed.
