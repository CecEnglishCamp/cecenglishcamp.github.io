미국 이웃과 첫 대화

First Conversation with an American Neighbor

🟡 한글 이야기
영자가 복도에서 이웃 Linda를 만났습니다.
"Hey! You must be the new neighbor! I\u0027m Linda!"
"Hello! I am Young-ja. Nice to meet you."
"How are you settling in?"
영자가 당황했습니다. 'Settling in?'
"I... am okay. Little by little."
"Ha! That\u0027s how everyone starts! If you need anything, just knock!"
영자가 사전을 찾았습니다. "Settling in = 새 환경에 적응하다."
"다음엔 I\u0027m settling in well, thank you!라고 하자."
🔤 영어 스토리

Young-ja meets her neighbor Linda. 🔊

"How are you settling in?" Linda asks. 🔊

Young-ja does not know this phrase. 🔊

"I am okay. Little by little." 🔊

"If you need anything, just knock!" 🔊

Young-ja looks up "settling in." 🔊

It means getting used to a new place. 🔊

Young-ja bumped into Linda in the hallway. 🔊

Linda smiled. "Hey! You must be the new neighbor!" 🔊

"Hello! I\u0027m Young-ja. Nice to meet you." 🔊

"How are you settling in?" 🔊

Young-ja\u0027s smile froze. She knew "how are you" but not "settling in." 🔊

"I... am okay. Little by little." 🔊

Linda laughed. "That\u0027s how everyone starts! Just knock if you need anything!" 🔊

Back home, Young-ja searched the phrase. 🔊

She wrote in her notebook: "Next time: I\u0027m settling in well, thank you!" 🔊
