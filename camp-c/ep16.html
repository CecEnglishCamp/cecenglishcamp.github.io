오늘의 이야기 — 한글 스토리
KOREAN STORY

“엄마, 아프면 병원 가세요. 여긴 한국 아니에요!”

딸의 말에 영자는 용기 내어 미국 병원 예약을 했습니다.

병원 접수대 앞에서 직원이 물었습니다.

“Insurance?”

영자는 당당하게 말했습니다.

“Sure! I’m in…surance!”

직원은 어리둥절, 영자는 미소 활짝.

하지만 그 미소는 곧 사라졌습니다.

“Your co-pay is 50 dollars.”

“코…뭐요?”

진료실로 들어가자 의사는 인사를 건넸습니다.

“Hi, how are you feeling?”

영자는 반사적으로 외쳤습니다.

“I’m fine, thank you. And you?”

의사는 웃으며, “No, I mean, where does it hurt?”

그제야 영자는 아픈 허리를 부여잡으며 말했습니다.

“My back… hurts… like kimchi fridge fell on me.”

의사는 웃으며 “That’s serious!”라고 말했지만,

진료비를 들은 영자는 속으로 외쳤습니다:

“진짜 심각한 건 이 금액이야…!”

✅ A2 Level

Young-ja visits a clinic in America for the first time.

At the front desk, they ask for her insurance.

She proudly says, “I’m in-surance!”

The receptionist looks confused.

The co-pay is $50, and she is surprised.

In the doctor’s room, she answers, “I’m fine, thank you,”

but the doctor actually wants to know what hurts.

She says, “My back hurts like kimchi fridge fell on me.”

The doctor laughs. The bill comes. Young-ja almost cries.

✅ B1 Level

It was Young-ja’s first time at an American clinic.

She walked in confidently, but then came the paperwork.

“Insurance?” asked the receptionist.

Young-ja smiled brightly, “Of course! I’m in-surance!”

The receptionist paused. The co-pay was $50. Ouch.

In the doctor’s office, he greeted her,

“How are you feeling today?”

Young-ja replied out of habit, “I’m fine, thank you. And you?”

The doctor chuckled. “No, really. What’s wrong?”

She held her back and said, “It hurts like a kimchi fridge fell on me.”

The doctor laughed. The treatment helped.

But when she saw the bill, Young-ja whispered,

“I think my wallet is also hurt…”
