1. 기본 개념 (Basic Concept)

QTFQUANTIFIERS

수량을 나타내는 표현 → much / many / few / little
명사의 종류(셀 수 있는/없는)에 따라 선택이 달라짐
긍정적 뉘앙스 vs 부정적 뉘앙스를 구분하여 사용

많음: I have many books / I have much time

긍정 뉘앙스: I have a few friends (몇 명 있음)

부정 뉘앙스: I have few friends (거의 없음)

긍정 뉘앙스: We have a little time (조금 있음)

부정 뉘앙스: We have little time (거의 없음)

셀 수 있는 명사 vs 셀 수 없는 명사

Countable (셀 수 있는)

many / few / a few

books, people, ideas, chairs

many books

a few ideas

few people

Uncountable (셀 수 없는)

much / little / a little

water, time, money, information

much water

a little time

little money

2. 문법 규칙 (Grammar Rules)

셀 수 있는 명사 (Countable Nouns)

  • many → 긍정/의문/부정문에서 사용
  • few → 부정적 느낌 (거의 없다)
  • a few → 긍정적 느낌 (조금 있다)
  • 예시: books, chairs, people, ideas, questions

셀 수 없는 명사 (Uncountable Nouns)

  • much → 주로 의문/부정문에서 사용
  • little → 부정적 느낌 (거의 없다)
  • a little → 긍정적 느낌 (조금 있다)
  • 예시: water, time, money, information, effort

강조 표현과 패턴

  • 강조 표현: so many, too much, very few, very little
  • 긍정 뉘앙스: a few / a little → 적지만 있음
  • 부정 뉘앙스: few / little → 거의 없음
  • 명사 종류를 먼저 판단하고 적절한 수량사 선택

3. 예시 문장 (Example Sentences)

many / few / a few (셀 수 있는 명사)

  • I have many books.
  • She has few friends. (거의 없음)
  • He has a few ideas. (몇 개 있음)
  • They brought many chairs.
  • We saw a few people at the park.
  • There are few students in the class.
  • She asked many questions.
  • I have a few pencils in my bag.
  • He knows few answers.
  • We invited many guests.
  • She made a few mistakes.
  • There are few options left.

much / little / a little (셀 수 없는 명사)

  • I don't have much time.
  • She has little money. (거의 없음)
  • We have a little water. (조금 있음)
  • He gave much effort to the project.
  • There is little hope left.
  • I need a little help.
  • They have much experience.
  • She showed little interest.
  • We have a little sugar left.
  • He has much knowledge.
  • There is little information available.
  • I have a little patience left.

4. 의문문 예시 (Question Examples)

How many...? (셀 수 있는 명사)
  • How many books do you have?
  • How many people came to the party?
  • How many questions are there?
  • How many chairs do we need?
How much...? (셀 수 없는 명사)
  • How much time do you have?
  • How much money did it cost?
  • How much water do you need?
  • How much experience do you have?
Do you have many/much...?
  • Do you have many friends?
  • Do you have much homework?
  • Are there many students here?
  • Is there much traffic today?
Are there few/little...?
  • Are there few options left?
  • Is there little hope?
  • Do you have few ideas?
  • Is there little time remaining?

5. 상황별 대화 (Situational Dialogues)

쇼핑 상황
A: How many apples do you want?
B: Just a few, please. Maybe three or four.
A: How much milk do you need?
B: Not much. A little is enough.
학교 상황
A: Do you have many classes today?
B: No, I have few classes. Only two.
A: How much homework do you have?
B: I have little homework today. Just math.
직장 상황
A: How many meetings do you have?
B: I have many meetings. About six today.
A: Do you have much work left?
B: Yes, I have much work to finish.
여행 상황
A: How many countries have you visited?
B: I've visited a few countries. Maybe five.
A: How much money do you need for the trip?
B: Not much. I saved a little money.

6. 문장 결합 (Combining Sentences)

간단한 문장을 복합문으로

• I have many books. + I have little time to read.
→ I have many books, but I have little time to read them.
• She has few friends. + She is happy.
→ Although she has few friends, she is happy.
• We have much work. + We need more people.
→ We have much work, so we need more people to help.
• He has a few ideas. + They are good ideas.
→ He has a few ideas, and they are all very good.
• There is little water. + We should save it.
→ Since there is little water, we should save it carefully.

8. 표현 패턴 (Expression Patterns)

자주 사용되는 표현 패턴

So many + countable

So many books to read!

So many people came!

Too much + uncountable

Too much noise!

Too much work to do!

Very few + countable

Very few students passed.

Very few options remain.

Very little + uncountable

Very little time left.

Very little money saved.

Quite a few = many

Quite a few people called.

Quite a few problems occurred.

Quite a bit = much

Quite a bit of work done.

Quite a bit of time saved.
